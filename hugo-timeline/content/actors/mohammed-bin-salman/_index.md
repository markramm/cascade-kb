## Biography

Mohammed bin Salman is the Crown Prince and Prime Minister of Saudi Arabia and its de facto ruler. He consolidated control around 2017 through a purge of rival princes, and he chairs the Council of Economic and Development Affairs and the kingdom's sovereign wealth vehicle, the Public Investment Fund (PIF). His signature initiative is Vision 2030, a state plan to diversify the Saudi economy away from oil.

In the capture-and-accountability context, MBS is significant as a foreign autocrat who built financial relationships with figures in Donald Trump's orbit. Most prominently, the Public Investment Fund — which he chairs — invested $2 billion in Affinity Partners, the private-equity firm founded by Jared Kushner after Kushner left the Trump White House. PIF later joined a consortium, alongside Affinity Partners and Silver Lake, in the roughly $55 billion leveraged buyout of Electronic Arts announced in 2025 — a deal that drew congressional scrutiny over the Kushner conflict of interest. These sovereign-wealth ties have raised persistent questions about the transactional basis of U.S. Middle East policy.

MBS led the Saudi-led military coalition in Yemen, where U.S.-supplied weapons were repeatedly tied to strikes on civilians and on Doctors Without Borders (MSF) hospitals, documented by Human Rights Watch, Amnesty International, and MSF. In 2018, the CIA assessed with high confidence that MBS ordered the murder of Washington Post journalist Jamal Khashoggi.
