## Biography

Scott Bessent was born in 1962 in Laurens, South Carolina, and graduated from Yale University with a B.A. in Political Science in 1984. He spent more than two decades in macro hedge funds, including a stint at Brown Brothers Harriman, before joining Soros Fund Management as Chief Investment Officer in 2011. At Soros he managed one of the world's largest macro portfolios — the firm George Soros founded and through which Soros made his famous 1992 short of the British pound. Bessent left in 2015.

That same year he founded Key Square Group, a global macro hedge fund that raised roughly $4.5 billion at launch — among the largest macro launches on record — anchored by a $2 billion investment from Soros Fund Management. Performance was mixed; the fund returned about 5% in its first year and faced outflows. By 2022 Key Square had wound down its external business and returned outside capital, with Bessent continuing to manage his own money through the vehicle. The Soros-to-Trump arc later drew attacks during the Republican primary and confirmation seasons from figures targeting "globalist" finance, which Bessent deflected by emphasizing his economic-nationalist "3-3-3" framework (3% GDP growth, 3% deficit reduction, 3 million additional barrels of oil per day).

Trump nominated Bessent in November 2024, and the Senate confirmed him on a party-line vote in January 2025. At nomination Bessent filed OGE Form 278 disclosures placing his net worth in an estimated $700M–$1B+ range (OGE ranges are coarse and specific figures were undisclosed), and disclosed a $250,001–$500,000 IBIT position he agreed to divest. As Treasury Secretary he chairs CFIUS, the interagency committee that reviews foreign investments in U.S. businesses for national-security risk. His tenure has included signing the March 2, 2025 Treasury announcement (release sb0038) suspending Corporate Transparency Act enforcement against U.S. citizens and domestic companies, and the March 21, 2025 companion release (sb0060) accompanying FinCEN's rule exempting U.S. entities from beneficial-ownership reporting — actions that drew a bipartisan demand for the legal basis from Senators Sheldon Whitehouse (D-RI) and Chuck Grassley (R-IA) on March 10, 2025.

Bessent is also a named implementer of the Strategic Bitcoin Reserve (Executive Order 14233, March 6, 2025), the primary Treasury-side architect of GENIUS Act stablecoin implementation (Public Law 119-27, signed July 18, 2025), and a member of the Presidential Council of Advisers on Digital Assets. In December 2025, at the New York Times DealBook Summit, he said the administration planned to "veto" regional Federal Reserve bank presidents who had not lived in their district for three years — a requirement the Federal Reserve Act does not contain; the Fed Board responded by unanimously reappointing 11 of its 12 regional presidents on December 11, 2025 (the 12th seat reflecting Atlanta's Raphael Bostic's previously announced retirement, not a refusal). On February 13, 2026, Senators Elizabeth Warren (D-MA) and Andy Kim (D-NJ) wrote to Bessent demanding to know whether CFIUS would review the $500 million Aryam Investment 1 / World Liberty Financial transaction tied to UAE figure Sheikh Tahnoon bin Zayed Al Nahyan — a deal whose January 2025 tranche distributed $187 million to Trump family entities. The March 5, 2026 deadline passed without a documented public response.
