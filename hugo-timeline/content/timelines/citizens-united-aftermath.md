Before *Citizens United v. FEC*, corporations and unions could not spend unlimited general-treasury funds on independent political communications. After it — January 21, 2010 — they could. The 5–4 majority opinion was authored by Justice Anthony Kennedy and joined by John Roberts, Antonin Scalia, Samuel Alito, and Clarence Thomas.

This timeline tracks what fifteen years of that ruling produced: the rise of super PACs, the explosion of dark-money networks, the operational coordination between donor-advised funds and the Federalist Society's judicial pipeline, and the legal challenges that further expanded — never narrowed — the doctrine.
