The Federalist Society was founded at Yale and Chicago law schools in 1982. Forty years later, six of its members held a majority on the Supreme Court. This is not coincidence; it is the longest-running successful institutional capture project in American history, with a single named operative — Leonard Leo — orchestrating the pipeline from membership recruitment through judicial nomination through dark-money confirmation campaigns.

Each justice's row begins with their nomination. Read across to see the rulings that followed. The density of post-2017 decisions on the lower rows shows what a captured Court does once installed.
