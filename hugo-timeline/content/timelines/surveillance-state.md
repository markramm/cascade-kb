Edward Snowden's June 2013 disclosures revealed the existence of the NSA's bulk telephone metadata collection program (Section 215), the PRISM program targeting nine major tech platforms, and the worldwide signals intelligence apparatus. The political reaction was significant: the USA FREEDOM Act of 2015 ended bulk collection of domestic phone metadata. That was, in retrospect, the only durable reform.

In the decade since, every other authority — Section 702, Executive Order 12333, the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court — has been renewed, expanded, or privatized. Palantir has become a billion-dollar government contractor; Booz Allen Hamilton (where Snowden worked) is publicly traded with the CIA's investment arm In-Q-Tel as an early backer. The 2025 administration installed a Director of National Intelligence (Tulsi Gabbard) and FBI Director (Kash Patel) explicitly committed to using these authorities against domestic political enemies.
