Reuters revealed in 2024 that SpaceX had entered a classified $1.8 billion contract with the National Reconnaissance Office (NRO) in 2021 to build hundreds of spy satellites functioning as a swarm for continuous real-time monitoring of targets around the globe. The Starshield program is a military-specific version of SpaceX's Starlink satellite internet network.

By April 2025, at least 183 Starshield satellites had been launched, with the constellation expanding rapidly. SpaceX subsequently secured $733.5 million in additional national security space mission contracts spanning the Space Development Agency and NRO (2025-2026). The Pentagon's Golden Dome missile tracking constellation — up to 600 satellites — is expected to add approximately $2 billion more.

SpaceX holds $22 billion in cumulative federal contracts across NASA, Space Force, NRO, and SDA, with $11.8 billion in remaining active contract value. The Starshield constellation represents the space-based surveillance layer of the Thiel-PayPal Mafia network: Musk's satellites provide persistent global coverage, Palantir's AI processes the intelligence, and Anduril's autonomous weapons act on it. All three companies are now jointly developing Golden Dome, bringing the full stack — satellite surveillance, AI analytics, and autonomous weapons — under a single integrated command architecture.
