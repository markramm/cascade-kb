On June 5, 2017, Saudi Arabia, the United Arab Emirates, Bahrain, and Egypt announced a comprehensive economic and diplomatic blockade of Qatar — closing land borders, suspending air links, and demanding Qatar accept 13 ultimatums including shutting Al Jazeera. The blockade lasted until January 2021. Within hours of the announcement, President Donald Trump publicly tweeted support for the blockade, and Jared Kushner — operating from his Senior Adviser role — backed the blockade through the National Security Council process **against the explicit advice of Secretary of State Rex Tillerson, Secretary of Defense James Mattis, and the career State Department**.

The blockade arrives **approximately six weeks after Charles Kushner's April 2017 trip to Doha seeking $1 billion in Qatari financing for 666 Fifth Avenue was rejected** ([[2017-04-15--charles-kushner-qatar-1b-request-rejected]]). The temporal sequence is the *evidentiary core* of the Phase 2 transfer-mechanism per the Abramson-thesis arc framework ([[epstein-kushner-gulf-sovereign-18-year-arc-abramson-thesis]]):

1. **April 2017**: Qatar refuses Kushner family's $1B 666 Fifth ask
2. **May 2017**: Kushner forms personal rapport with MBS in Riyadh ([[2017-05-20--trump-riyadh-trip-kushner-mbs-rapport-begins]])
3. **June 5, 2017**: Saudi-UAE blockade Qatar; Kushner backs against Cabinet
4. **August 3, 2018**: QIA-Brookfield bails out 666 Fifth for $1.28B ([[2018-08-03--kushner-qatar-brookfield-bailout]])

Whether the sequence reflects active coercion (Abramson's strong-form reading), structural-policy-misalignment-with-Cabinet (the institutionalist reading), or coincidence (the most generous reading), the **structural fact** is that a Senior Adviser to the President with personal-family-financial-interest exposure to Qatar drove U.S. foreign policy against Qatar in a way that the Cabinet opposed, and 14 months later Qatar reversed its position via the bailout that resolved the family-financial-interest exposure. The blockade and the bailout are not separable events — they are the two halves of the Phase 2 transfer-mechanism.

The Cabinet-override pattern itself is structurally significant: a routine Senior-Adviser role does not normally override the Secretary of State and Secretary of Defense on a major foreign-policy alliance question. The override in this case is a marker of the operational priority placed on the Saudi-UAE relationship by the Trump-Kushner principal channel — the same priority that would manifest in October 2017 (Ritz-Carlton arrests) and October 2018 (Khashoggi response).
