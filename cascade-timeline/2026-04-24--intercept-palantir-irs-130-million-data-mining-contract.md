The Intercept reported that Palantir Technologies has been paid more than $130 million by the IRS to analyze sensitive federal databases, with the IRS Criminal Investigation Division using Palantir's Lead and Case Analytics platform to aggregate dozens of datasets on Americans for "massive-scale" data mining of financial-crime suspects. The IRS contract, first launched under Trump's first term and expanded under Biden, is now operated by an IRS Criminal Investigation office that — under Trump's second-term direction — has scaled back its pursuit of tax cheats and pivoted toward investigating "left-leaning groups" per the Intercept's reporting.

The IRS contract sits alongside Palantir's $300 million USDA "National Farm Security Action Plan" (April 22, 2026), which includes a $75M no-bid worker-surveillance component for tracking federal employee return-to-office compliance, and Palantir's $30 million ImmigrationOS contract for ICE. The Intercept's reporting documents Palantir's expansion from foreign-intelligence analytics to domestic federal-employee surveillance and IRS-criminal-investigation data mining — an architecture aligned with the [[2026-04-23--144-trump-appointees-palantir-disclosures]] disclosure pattern showing 144 Trump administration appointees with Palantir holdings, compensation, or employment ties. The cumulative federal contract footprint now visibly includes IRS, USDA, ICE, DOD, HHS, and OMB.
