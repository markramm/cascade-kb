Beginning November 17, 2025, board members of the Heritage Foundation—the conservative think tank that spearheaded Project 2025—resigned over controversy surrounding white nationalist Nick Fuentes.

The resignations followed Heritage Foundation President Kevin Roberts's decision to release a video defending Tucker Carlson's October 2025 interview with Fuentes. In the interview, Carlson gave Fuentes an overwhelmingly friendly platform to discuss his views, prompting intense criticism from some mainstream conservatives while others defended the appearance.

The board resignations represented a significant rupture within the conservative establishment over the explicit embrace of figures with documented white nationalist and Holocaust denial views. Fuentes, 26, leads the Groyper Army and has been banned from most social media platforms for antisemitic and racist content—though his X account was reinstated by Elon Musk and now has over 1 million followers.

The controversy highlighted competing visions within the conservative movement:

**Fuentes faction**: Argues the interview represents growing influence and mainstream acceptance. Fuentes suggested his followers have embedded themselves within the Trump administration, telling supporters to infiltrate Project 2025, AFPI, American Moment, and the Trump campaign.

**Traditional conservatives**: View explicit white nationalism as beyond acceptable bounds. One Heritage board member who resigned said Roberts's defense of the interview represented a betrayal of the foundation's principles.

**Roberts's position**: Defended Carlson's interview as legitimate engagement with ideas, declining to condemn Fuentes directly.

The incident demonstrated the tension between:
- The Heritage Foundation's role as architect of Project 2025 (the policy blueprint for Trump's second term)
- Growing influence of explicit white nationalists within the MAGA coalition
- Pressure from Groyper-aligned activists to mainstream their movement

New York Times columnist Michelle Goldberg wrote that the controversy signaled Fuentes's politics were "on the rise" within the Republican Party. JD Vance, now Vice President, called Fuentes a "total loser"—but the controversy persisted within Heritage itself.

This represented the most significant public rupture within the conservative policy infrastructure over white nationalism since the formation of the modern right-wing network in the 1970s-1980s. Unlike the 1965 Buckley purge of the John Birch Society, this controversy did not result in clear ideological boundaries—Roberts remained as Heritage president despite the resignations.
