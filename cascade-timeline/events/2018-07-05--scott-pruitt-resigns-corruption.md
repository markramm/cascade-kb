EPA Administrator Scott Pruitt resigned facing at least 14 federal investigations 
into corruption, ethics violations, and misuse of taxpayer funds. Scandals included: 
spending $43,000 on a soundproof phone booth, first-class travel costing over 
$100,000, using staff for personal errands like finding his wife a job and getting 
a Chick-fil-A franchise, renting a Capitol Hill condo from a lobbyist for $50/night, 
24/7 security detail costing $3 million, and bypassing White House to give raises 
to favored aides. As Oklahoma AG, Pruitt had sued the EPA 14 times before leading 
it, epitomizing regulatory capture.
