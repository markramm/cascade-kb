Steve Bannon turned sharply against Vice President JD Vance, igniting what observers described as "fresh MAGA fireworks" in the ongoing conservative civil war. The attack represented a dramatic reversal for Bannon, who had previously supported Vance's rise and Senate campaign.

The fracturing of Trump's coalition saw Bannon, the self-appointed guardian of populist-nationalist ideology, now targeting the sitting Vice President as insufficiently loyal to America First principles. Coming just days after Bannon's attacks on Ben Shapiro at AmericaFest, the Vance attack signaled that no figure in the MAGA movement was immune from purge as various factions positioned themselves for the 2028 succession battle.

The intra-movement warfare highlighted the instability of a political coalition held together primarily by personal loyalty to Trump rather than coherent ideology. As different factions competed to define the movement's post-Trump future, the resulting chaos threatened to fragment the right-wing coalition that had achieved unified government control.
