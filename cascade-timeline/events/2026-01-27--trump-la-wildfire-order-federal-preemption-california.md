President Trump signed an executive order directing aggressive federal intervention to accelerate rebuilding in Los Angeles neighborhoods devastated by the 2025 Pacific Palisades and Eaton Canyon wildfires, explicitly asserting that California and local officials failed to manage the fires or enable reconstruction. The order instructs FEMA and the SBA to issue regulations within 30 days that preempt state and local procedural permitting requirements, replacing them with federally supervised self-certification by builders. It also requires agencies to fast-track environmental and historic preservation reviews, propose legislation to handle future cases where states "impede disaster recovery," and conduct audits of California's nearly $3 billion in unspent Hazard Mitigation Grant Program funds.

The order represents an extraordinary assertion of federal supremacy over state and local building regulations, potentially establishing precedent for federal override of local democratic governance in disaster recovery. Nearly 40,000 acres burned in the fires, killing at least 31 people and destroying 16,000 structures, with economic losses estimated as high as $131 billion. Governor Gavin Newsom's office argued that more than 1,625 home permits had been issued with timelines "at least 2x faster than before the fires," and urged the federal government to release additional disaster funding rather than override state authority. Legal experts predict the order will face immediate court challenges over constitutional federalism questions and presidential authority to unilaterally preempt state building codes and environmental laws.
