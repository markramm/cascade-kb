Former CNN host Don Lemon was arrested in Beverly Hills, California on January 30, 2026, on federal charges stemming from a grand jury indictment. The charge: **conspiracy against religious freedom**.

**Prior judicial rejection:**
On January 22, 2026 — just eight days before the arrest — federal magistrate Judge Douglas Micko had rejected an earlier complaint against Lemon, finding insufficient basis for the charges. Rather than accept the judicial determination, prosecutors went to a grand jury to obtain an indictment, bypassing the magistrate's assessment.

**Context:** Lemon, who was fired from CNN in April 2023, had been covering the Minneapolis crisis and immigration enforcement actions. His arrest was widely characterized as part of the administration's pattern of journalist suppression alongside the rescission of journalist protection policies, the Bolton raid, and the Schiff investigation.

The "conspiracy against religious freedom" charge — an obscure Reconstruction-era statute rarely used in modern prosecutions — raised immediate First Amendment concerns from press freedom organizations.
