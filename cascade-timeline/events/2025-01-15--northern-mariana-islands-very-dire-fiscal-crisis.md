notes: 'GAO report reveals CNMI in "very dire" fiscal condition. Real GDP down 35% from fiscal 2017 to 2022. Tourism still down 55% from 2019 levels despite recovery from 12,700 visitors in 2021 (from 660,000 in 2017). 2018 typhoon and 2020 largest casino closure devastated economy. Public debt $121.1 million (13% of GDP), net pension liabilities 49% of GDP as of September 30, 2021. Pension plan bankrupt since 2012, territory taking loans to make payments. CNMI officials tell GAO "uncertain how they will continue to make expected debt service and pension fund payments." With tourism struggling, federal assistance slowing/expiring, and weak financial management, "CNMI is at risk of severe fiscal crisis." Bonds unrated, hoping to issue by January 2026 after releasing delayed FY2022-2023 ACFRs.'
