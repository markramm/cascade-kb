Following the acquittal of Los Angeles police officers in the Rodney King beating and the subsequent civil unrest, the Immigration and Naturalization Service collaborates with the LAPD to conduct sweeping arrests targeting undocumented immigrants among those detained during the riots. INS officials report processing over 1,000 individuals for deportation, with many facing expedited removal without full hearings. The enforcement operation treats immigration status as a marker of criminality, subjecting foreign-born arrestees to detention and deportation while U.S. citizens arrested for identical offenses face standard criminal processing.

The post-riot deportation campaign exemplifies "crisis immigration enforcement"—using civil emergencies to justify expanded enforcement against immigrant communities. Governor Pete Wilson, who later champions Proposition 187, calls for federal assistance in deportation efforts, framing undocumented immigrants as responsible for urban unrest despite evidence that participants in the riots reflected the diverse demographics of South Central Los Angeles. Media coverage emphasizing arrests of Central American immigrants contributes to racialized narratives associating immigration with crime and disorder.

The 1992 Los Angeles enforcement operation establishes precedents for subsequent crisis-driven deportation campaigns. After natural disasters, terrorist attacks, and civil disturbances, immigration enforcement agencies leverage emergency conditions to conduct operations that would face greater scrutiny under normal circumstances. The pattern—using crisis to justify enforcement against vulnerable populations with diminished access to legal process—recurs through subsequent decades, from post-9/11 special registration targeting Muslim immigrants to post-hurricane enforcement against displaced workers. The LA riots deportations demonstrate how immigration enforcement serves as a mechanism for managing racialized populations during moments of social rupture, providing authorities with tools to remove inconvenient witnesses and potential complainants while appearing to restore order.
