On March 13, 2026, more than 120 House Democrats, led by Representative Sara Jacobs of California, sent a formal letter to Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth demanding a detailed accounting of artificial intelligence's role in selecting the Minab girls' school as a strike target. The February 28 attack killed more than 170 people, including over 100 children, and had become the most scrutinized single incident of the Iran war. The lawmakers specifically asked whether the Maven Smart System — the Pentagon's AI-powered targeting platform operated by Palantir — had identified the school as a military facility.

The letter posed a series of pointed questions about the targeting pipeline: what data sources fed the AI system's assessment, whether human operators had overridden or simply approved the machine's recommendation, and what confidence threshold the system had assigned to the school's classification as an IRGC command post. The Democrats demanded answers by March 20, a timeline the Pentagon would ultimately not meet. Jacobs told NBC News that the incident raised "fundamental questions about whether we are allowing machines to make life-and-death decisions faster than humans can meaningfully review them."

The demand intersected directly with the ongoing Anthropic dispute. Anthropic's refusal to allow Claude to be used in autonomous targeting workflows — one of the red lines that precipitated its blacklisting — took on new significance in light of the Minab strike. Critics noted that the Pentagon was simultaneously punishing a company for insisting on human oversight of AI-driven targeting while facing questions about whether inadequate oversight had contributed to the deadliest civilian casualty event of the war. The Pentagon said it would respond to the letter through appropriate channels but offered no public timeline.
