On March 3, 2026, Iranian drones struck the U.S. Embassy compound in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, in one of the most significant escalations since Operation Epic Fury began on February 28. Two suspected Iranian drones hit the embassy compound, causing minor material damage. The building was empty at the time of the attack and no embassy staff casualties were reported, though the strike demonstrated Iran's ability and intent to strike American diplomatic facilities in allied Gulf states.

Separately, the Washington Post reported that the CIA station housed within the Riyadh compound was also hit in the attack, representing an intelligence facility strike with potentially significant operational consequences. The U.S. Mission to Saudi Arabia immediately issued a "shelter in place" alert covering Riyadh, Jeddah, and Dhahran. The State Department closed U.S. embassies in Saudi Arabia and Kuwait in the immediate aftermath.

The Riyadh strike was part of a broader wave of Iranian retaliation that included drone and ballistic missile attacks on U.S. military bases in Qatar, Bahrain, the UAE, Kuwait, Iraq, Jordan, and Saudi Arabia. By March 5, a military source confirmed Iran had fired over 500 ballistic and naval missiles and approximately 2,000 drones since February 28, with roughly 40 percent aimed at Israel and 60 percent at U.S. targets in the region.

The attack on the Riyadh embassy intensified the regional scope of the conflict and underscored the risk that Operation Epic Fury — framed by the Trump administration as a contained campaign targeting Iranian military capabilities — was pulling multiple countries into active hostilities. Iran's willingness to strike diplomatic compounds in allied Gulf states represented a significant threshold crossing, raising the prospect of broader Gulf state involvement and putting the Trump administration's assurances of a controlled campaign under immediate pressure.
