On March 19, 2026, data revealed that federal Inspector General offices had lost 16.6% of their workforce — a rate significantly exceeding the 12% overall reduction in the civil service. The disproportionate cuts to the government's internal watchdog infrastructure represented a systematic assault on the oversight mechanisms designed to detect waste, fraud, and abuse across federal agencies.

The workforce gutting was compounded by Trump's nomination of openly partisan figures to fill IG vacancies. Anthony D'Esposito, a former Republican congressman from New York, was nominated to serve as the Department of Labor's Inspector General despite having no background in auditing, investigations, or oversight. The nomination of a political ally to a position designed to be independent drew sharp criticism from government accountability organizations, who argued it violated the Inspector General Act's requirement for appointees chosen based on integrity and demonstrated ability.

More alarming still was the testimony of Thomas March Bell, Trump's nominee for the Health and Human Services Inspector General, who told lawmakers during his confirmation process that he would investigate "to support the initiatives" of the President. The statement represented a fundamental misunderstanding — or deliberate rejection — of the IG's role as an independent watchdog. IGs are statutorily mandated to serve as objective investigators accountable to both agency heads and Congress, not as instruments of presidential policy.

The administration simultaneously proposed cutting IG funding by 30%, which would further cripple the offices' capacity to conduct audits and investigations. In a particularly aggressive move, the Department of Homeland Security's general counsel had requested a list of all ongoing IG investigations — a demand that threatened the confidentiality essential to effective oversight work. Taken together, the workforce reductions, partisan nominations, budget cuts, and direct interference constituted the most comprehensive attack on the federal inspector general system since its creation in 1978.
