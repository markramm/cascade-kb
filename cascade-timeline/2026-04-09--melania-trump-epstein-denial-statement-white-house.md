First Lady Melania Trump delivered a rare on-camera statement from the White House on April 9 denying ties to Jeffrey Epstein. She declared "I am not Epstein's victim" and "Epstein did not introduce me to Donald Trump," stated she first met Epstein in 2000 at an event she attended with Trump, dismissed email correspondence with Ghislaine Maxwell as "trivial," and called on Congress to provide Epstein survivors with public hearings and sworn testimony entered into the Congressional Record.

The statement stunned White House aides who had minimal advance notice. Trump told the New York Times he did not know the details of what Melania planned to say, stating "I didn't recommend it, but I let it be her." Analysts noted the statement upended a news cycle dominated by Iran peace negotiations and dragged the Epstein saga -- which Trump had been desperate to move past -- back into the spotlight. CNN analysis argued the statement "tried to sweep the Epstein saga away, but she ended up reviving it."

The unaddressed context: the man who introduced Trump to Melania in 1998 was Paolo Zampolli, an Italian-American modeling agent who now serves as Trump's Special Representative for Global Partnerships. Zampolli's ID Model Management was patronized by Epstein, and Zampolli partnered with Epstein in a 2004 bid to purchase Elite Model Management. Zampolli's name appears in the Epstein files, including a 2011 email where Epstein warned an Emirati businessman that "zampoli [sic] is trouble." Amanda Ungaro, a model in Zampolli's orbit, had been brought to New York on Epstein's plane from Paris by Jean-Luc Brunel, an alleged procurer for Epstein. The Trump-Melania introduction thus occurred through a modeling agent directly connected to the Epstein recruitment network -- a fact Melania's statement did not address.
