# NWCCU Splits From C-RAC, Moves to Rebrand as National Accreditor

On June 15, 2026, the **Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities (NWCCU)** announced it is leaving **C-RAC** — the Council on Recognized Accrediting Commissions, the coordinating body of the regional accreditors — as part of a broader move to reshape itself as a *national* accreditor. The NWCCU president framed the departure as part of an effort to "focus on national engagement where it can have the greatest impact," paired with a planned name change and a revision of its accrediting standards. In February 2026 the NWCCU Board had authorized its president to explore the name change. The Chronicle of Higher Education characterized the move as the accreditor cutting ties with its "historical clique."

## Why it is structurally significant

Accreditation is a documented capture vector in the higher-education-media-capture lane. The load-bearing precondition is the **2020 U.S. Department of Education policy change that removed regional designations among accreditors** — once accreditors are no longer bound to a region, institutions can shop nationally for friendlier oversight, and accreditors can compete for institutions by relaxing or re-pitching standards.

NWCCU's move is the **supply-side** instance of that de-regionalization: an established regional accreditor declaring itself national and rewriting its standards. It is the structural counterpart to the **demand-side** instance already documented in this timeline — DeSantis's Commission for Public Higher Education (CPHE), the six-state Southeastern accreditor built explicitly to give aligned public systems an alternative to SACSCOC (see `2026-06-06--desantis-cphe-six-state-accreditor-first-accreditations-target`). Both moves exploit the same post-2020 de-regionalized accreditation market; together they describe a system in which oversight is increasingly a thing institutions and political actors can choose rather than a constraint imposed on them.

This is convergence, not coordination: there is no evidence NWCCU and CPHE are acting together. They are independently exploiting the same structural opening — which is precisely what makes the de-regionalization the load-bearing claim.
