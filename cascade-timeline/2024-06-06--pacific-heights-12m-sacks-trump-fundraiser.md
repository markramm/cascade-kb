# David Sacks Hosts $12M Trump Pacific Heights Fundraiser — JD Vance as Operational Instigator, Six Weeks Before VP Selection

## Scene

On the evening of June 6, 2024, former President Donald Trump attended a campaign fundraiser at the Pacific Heights mansion of venture capitalist David Sacks, co-hosted by fellow All-In Podcast host Chamath Palihapitiya and Sacks's wife Jacqueline. The event raised approximately $12 million for Trump's campaign, confirmed by Republican attorney Harmeet Dhillon. Ticket prices ran from $50,000 per person at the entry level to $300,000 for a top-tier package including a photo with Trump, with some couples paying $500,000. The event sold out. Roughly 25 guests attended the seated dinner inside the mansion; a second reception cohort of approximately 50 brought the total to roughly 75 attendees in a multi-block security perimeter established by Secret Service and SFPD. Named attendees confirmed in contemporaneous reporting included Sen. JD Vance (R-OH), Sen. Bill Hagerty (R-TN), Cameron and Tyler Winklevoss (crypto entrepreneurs), Jacob Helberg (Palantir senior policy adviser, who disclosed approximately $1 million in personal Trump-campaign donations at the event), and Harmeet Dhillon. Elon Musk was reported as a likely attendee by at least one donor but was not independently confirmed present.

At the event, Sacks gave remarks that publicly named Vance as the operational instigator: *"This all started with JD Vance calling and asking if we could host an event for President Trump. Without JD's advice and encouragement, this would never have happened."* Sacks had also endorsed Trump publicly via social media hours before the dinner, citing economic policy, foreign policy, border policy, and legal fairness as reasons Trump outperformed Biden in what Sacks called an "A/B test." Both the fundraiser and the endorsement — formally attributed to Vance's initiative — were the same-day public culmination of a six-month Vance-orchestrated Silicon Valley donor-acquisition campaign.

## Context: Vance-Sacks Coordination Arc and the Pre-VP Window

The Pacific Heights fundraiser was the operational summit of a six-month campaign that Vance had been running to route Silicon Valley capital and credibility into the Trump orbit. According to an Axios scoop published May 24, 2024 — thirteen days before the event — Vance had spent the prior six months lobbying tech investors to support Trump, treating the San Francisco fundraiser as his "crown jewel." The mechanics were documented: Vance introduced Sacks to Donald Trump Jr., and the three met in Washington on March 6, 2024, following a dinner with Republican lawmakers at the Conrad hotel — a documented physical coordination meeting preceding the fundraiser by three months.

Sacks confirmed Vance's organizing role to Axios before the event: *"J.D. has been instrumental in making the Trump event happen."* The Axios reporting also noted that Vance was eyeing additional fundraising events in Los Angeles, Chicago, and Miami and was continuing to solicit tech-figure endorsements for Trump.

The June 6 event occurred six weeks before the Republican National Convention's July 15, 2024 announcement of Vance as Trump's VP pick.
