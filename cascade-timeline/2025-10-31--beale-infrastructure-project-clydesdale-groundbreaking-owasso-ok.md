## Opening

Beale Infrastructure, the Blue Owl Capital portfolio company, broke ground on October 30/31, 2025 on Project Clydesdale — a $1 billion data center campus in Owasso, Tulsa County, Oklahoma (northeast of Tulsa). The 506-acre site is Beale's first completed groundbreaking in Oklahoma and was announced via BusinessWire on October 31, 2025.

## What Happened / Key Facts

Project Clydesdale occupies 506 acres in Owasso, Oklahoma. The campus consists of at least one phase with three possible additional phases; each phase covers approximately 200,000 square feet. Construction is expected to take at least five years. The project represents a $1 billion capital investment and will generate approximately 100 permanent high-paying technical roles plus thousands of local construction jobs during build-out. Projected tax revenue: $2 million annually to Tulsa County and Owasso Public Schools once operational. Tenant information was not disclosed publicly.

## Broader Context

The Clydesdale groundbreaking preceded Beale's other Oklahoma proposals (Project Mustang in Claremore; the subsequently-withdrawn Project Atlas in Coweta). As of March 2026, Oklahoma Energy Today reported that Blue Owl Capital's financial challenges (stock crashed 68% from peak; active BDC redemption crisis) could affect Oklahoma datacenter project timelines — though no specific Clydesdale delays were confirmed at that time. Clydesdale appeared to remain under construction as of June 2026.
