On **June 30, 2026**, the Loudon County, Tennessee Commission voted to impose a **six-month moratorium** halting all county permits, zoning approvals, building approvals, utility extensions, and development agreements for data centers. A public hearing preceded the vote, with residents citing noise pollution, water consumption, power draw, and utility-cost concerns — the Channel B ratepayer pressure county bodies are increasingly naming explicitly. The Planning and Codes Director was directed to recommend zoning changes before the moratorium expires.

The recurring driver is explicit ratepayer/utility-cost concern, not just land-use; the open lead is what siting rules Loudon writes during the pause.
