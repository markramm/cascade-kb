President Donald Trump signed Executive Order 14338, "Improving Our Nation Through Better Design," on August 21, 2025, establishing the National Design Studio (NDS) within the White House Executive Office of the President as a temporary organization set to terminate three years from signing (August 21, 2028). The EO formally created the "America by Design" initiative to modernize federal digital and physical services.

**EO 14338 key provisions:**

- § 2(a): Establishes the NDS within the White House EOP; creates the Chief Design Officer position within NDS; the NDS Administrator "shall report to the Office of the White House Chief of Staff" (Susie Wiles).
- § 2 (temporary organization): Invokes 5 U.S.C. § 3161 hiring authorities to establish NDS as a temporary organization, granting hiring flexibilities outside standard civil-service constraints.
- § 3 (implementation): Agencies must consult with the Chief Design Officer and report initial implementation results by July 4, 2026; authorizes private-sector recruitment via Title IV of the Intergovernmental Personnel Act of 1970 (Public Law 91-648, 5 U.S.C. 3371 et seq.); directs the General Services Administrator to update the United States Web Design System.
- Scope: Targets approximately 26,000 federal web portals for redesign (per David Sacks, AI and crypto czar, as reported by Bloomberg).

**NDS leadership structure per EO:** The Administrator position (reporting to Chief of Staff) was not filled on signing date. Joe Gebbia (Airbnb co-founder, DOGE founding member February 2025) was appointed Chief Design Officer of the United States, confirmed publicly by Bloomberg August 21 and confirmed by Gebbia August 25, 2025.
