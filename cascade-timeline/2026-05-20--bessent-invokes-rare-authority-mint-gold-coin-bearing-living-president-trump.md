Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent invoked a statutory authority that empowers the Treasury Secretary to authorize 24-karat gold commemorative coins in order to bypass the federal-law prohibition on living presidents appearing on U.S. currency, advancing a gold coin bearing Donald Trump's image as part of the nation's 250th-anniversary commemorations. The U.S. Commission of Fine Arts — whose members were appointed by Trump earlier in the year — approved the coin's design without objection; the front depicts Trump in a suit leaning forward with his fists on a desk, with "LIBERTY" arced above and "1776–2026" beneath. Rep. Steve Cohen (TN-9) led a letter urging Treasury to halt the coin as a violation of federal law and congressional precedent, since portraits of living individuals are not permitted on U.S. currency.
