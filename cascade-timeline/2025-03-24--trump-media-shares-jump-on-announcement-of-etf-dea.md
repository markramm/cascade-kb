Trump Media & Technology Group, the company controlling Trump's Truth Social platform, announced a partnership with Crypto.com — a Singapore-based cryptocurrency exchange — to launch a series of "Made in America" exchange-traded funds (ETFs) on March 24, 2025. Trump Media's stock price (ticker: DJT) surged approximately 12% on the news. The planned ETFs, developed through Trump Media's financial services subsidiary Truth.Fi, would invest in American-focused assets including domestic energy companies, domestic manufacturing, and cryptocurrency. Crypto.com would serve as a custodian and operational partner for the new financial products.

The deal illustrated the expanding commercialization of Trump's political brand into financial products. Trump Media had struggled to generate revenue from Truth Social itself, reporting minimal advertising income in its public filings. The ETF venture represented an attempt to monetize the MAGA political brand through financial instruments marketed to Trump's base. The "Made in America" framing echoed Trump's political rhetoric, blurring the line between political messaging and commercial investment products. The partnership with a foreign-headquartered cryptocurrency exchange also raised questions about the stated "Made in America" positioning.

The stock price movement following the announcement reflected a broader pattern in which Trump Media shares functioned less as a conventional business equity and more as a political sentiment indicator — surging and falling based on Trump-related news rather than fundamental business performance. Critics noted that the Trump family's financial interests in Trump Media stock, held through a lockup period during which the president could not sell shares, created incentives for the administration to generate favorable publicity for the company regardless of its actual business prospects. The ETF announcement generated substantial press coverage that boosted the stock price even before any financial products were actually launched.
