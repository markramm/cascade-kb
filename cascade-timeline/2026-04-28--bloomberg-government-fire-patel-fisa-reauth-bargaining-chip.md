Bloomberg Government reported on April 28 that Democratic congressional leadership is offering Patel's removal as a bargaining chip in negotiations over FISA Section 702 reauthorization, with Rep. Ted Lieu (D-CA) signaling that firing Patel would help bring Democratic votes to a renewed surveillance-authority package. Section 702 — the warrantless-surveillance authority authorizing FBI access to NSA-collected foreign-intelligence data — sunset on April 20, 2026 under the 2024 Reforming Intelligence and Securing America Act (RISAA). A 10-day extension was enacted; current talks center on whether the House will renew without reform.

The bargaining-chip framing is structurally significant: under Patel's leadership, FBI "sensitive queries" — Section 702 searches targeting religious leaders, politicians, political candidates, or journalists — increased to more than three times 2024 levels. Patel's FBI also closed multiple oversight offices, fired compliance staff, and the administration dismantled the Privacy and Civil Liberties Oversight Board (PCLOB). Democratic negotiators are now openly using FISA renewal leverage to extract Patel's removal, while the administration uses Patel as a bargaining liability. The Politico exit signal (April 25), the Atlantic story (April 17), the SPLC indictment (April 21), and now the FISA reauthorization deal converge on Patel's removal as the central administrative question of late April.
