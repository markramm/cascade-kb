In 2017 — the year after JD Vance's memoir *Hillbilly Elegy* made him a national political figure — Charlie Kirk reached out via Twitter direct message to compliment Vance on a Tucker Carlson Fox News appearance. Vance later recalled: "A while ago, probably in 2017, I appeared on Tucker Carlson's Fox show to talk about God knows what. Afterwards a name I barely knew sent me a DM on twitter and told me I did a great job. It was Charlie Kirk, and that moment of kindness began a friendship that lasted until today." This documented origin of the Kirk-Vance friendship is the earliest-known contact between the two figures and the baseline for the Catholic-adjacent track in the Kirk coalition-building thesis.

## What Happened / Key Facts

Vance's primary-source account of the friendship's origin — posted to X/Twitter after Kirk's September 10, 2025 assassination and reported by The National Desk and NBC News — is unambiguous on three points: (1) the year was approximately 2017, (2) Kirk reached out first, and (3) the friendship began via social media after a Tucker Carlson Fox News appearance, not through political organizing.

The friendship's pre-political character matters for the Catholic-adjacent track analysis: Kirk was still a secular-libertarian conservative in 2017, not yet the Christian nationalist he would become after 2020. Vance was a newly famous memoirist, not yet a Senate candidate. The relationship preceded both men's full embrace of the Christian-nationalist coalition politics it would later support.

**Key relational facts documented in Tier-1 sourcing:**

- Kirk introduced Vance to Trump's network during the 2022 Ohio Senate campaign. Per The Hill (April 28, 2022), Kirk and Josh Hawley stumped for Vance in Ohio in the final days of the Republican primary — one of the earliest documented in-person political co-appearances.
- The two men engaged in ongoing theological debate: Vance (Catholic convert, 2019) and Kirk (evangelical Protestant) "used to argue about Catholicism and Protestantism and who was right about minor doctrinal questions," per Biography.com reporting.
- Kirk attended Mass with his Catholic wife and children, per multiple sources, and was moving toward Catholic conversion in the months before his death.

**The Catholic conversion trajectory:**
Per Angelus News (Diocese of Los Angeles, September 2025), citing testimony from Robert Brennan whose brother is Bishop Joseph V. Brennan of Fresno: Kirk attended a pro-life prayer breakfast in Visalia, California in the Diocese of Fresno "a little more than a week" before his September 10, 2025 assassination, and told Bishop Brennan when parting: "I'm _this_ close" to entering the Catholic Church. Kirk had made a video addressing Protestants about the Blessed Virgin Mary, and his final video focused on Mary as "the preeminent mediatrix of all time and space." These are documented markers of Catholic theological engagement, not merely cultural Catholicism through Erika's practice.

Kirk's religious evolution toward Catholic practice — documented by the Brennan testimony, the Marian video content, and regular Mass attendance — combined with the long-standing friendship with Vance (who converted to Catholicism in 2019 through the influence of Peter Thiel associate and Notre Dame professor Oren Cass-adjacent figures) constitutes the Catholic-adjacent track evidence base. Unlike the Reformed track (direct Wilson co-platform) and NAR track (direct Wallnau co-platform), the Catholic-adjacent track runs primarily through: (1) the personal friendship with Vance, (2) Erika Kirk's Catholic identity and practice, and (3) Kirk's own documented movement toward Catholic conversion.

**No documented co-platform appearances with Catholic-integralist intellectuals** (Sohrab Ahmari, Patrick Deneen, Adrian Vermeule, R.R. Reno) were found in the research for this task. The Catholic-integralist intellectual lane was not Kirk's direct orbit; the Catholic connection ran via friendship with Vance and personal religious evolution rather than through the Cluny/Gioberti/ACTS 17 network Vance was embedded in.

## Why This Event Matters

The 2017 Twitter DM establishes that the Kirk-Vance friendship predates both men's full Christian-nationalist commitments and the formal three-track coalition architecture. This timeline is significant for the downstream theme task ([[build-theme-kirk-as-original-three-tracks-bridge-erika-as-organizational-successor-vance-as-political-successor]]): Kirk was not instrumentally cultivating Vance as part of a calculated coalition-building strategy in 2017 — the friendship formed first, and then developed alongside both men's theological-political evolution.

The theological argument debates Vance and Kirk conducted about Catholicism vs. Protestantism — documented in sources citing friends of both men — represent the Catholic-adjacent track at its most personal level: Kirk was not institutionally accessing the Catholic-integralist network through Vance, but was in ongoing theological dialogue with a Catholic convert whose intellectual orbit included Vermeule, Deneen, and the post-liberal Catholic world. The friendship functioned as the substrate through which the Catholic register entered Kirk's coalition awareness.

The Brennan testimony about Kirk's near-conversion "a little more than a week" before his assassination represents the endpoint of a trajectory that the Vance friendship was part of catalyzing. Kirk's final video content — focused on Marian theology — positioned him closer to formal Catholic intellectual commitments than any of the Reformed or NAR speakers on his platforms.

## Broader Context

The National Catholic Register's coverage of Kirk's memorial as a religious event — noting that Vance recited the Nicene Creed, Pete Hegseth read from Ephesians, and Tucker Carlson spoke about divine providence — illustrates how the three-track coalition Kirk had built was visibly integrated at the moment of his death and memorial. The Catholic-adjacent track (Vance), the Reformed track (Hegseth-CREC), and the NAR/evangelical track (general evangelical memorial framing) all appeared at the State Farm Stadium memorial on September 21, 2025.

**Negative finding — Catholic-integralist intellectuals:** No documented co-platform appearances between Kirk and Ahmari, Deneen, Vermeule, or Reno were found. The Catholic-adjacent track for Kirk runs through (1) Vance personal friendship, (2) Erika's practice, and (3) Kirk's own pre-conversion trajectory — not through the integralist-intellectual wing that Vance was personally connected to via Cluny/Notre Dame networks. This distinction matters for the theme task: Kirk's Catholic-adjacent connection was devotional and relational, not intellectual-theological in the integralist register.
