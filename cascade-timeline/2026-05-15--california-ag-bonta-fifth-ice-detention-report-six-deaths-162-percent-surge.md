California Attorney General Rob Bonta released his fifth consecutive report on conditions at ICE detention facilities in California on May 15, 2026, finding conditions "cruel, inhumane, and unacceptable." The report documents six detainee deaths between September 2025 and March 2026 — the highest death toll since California's detention inspections began in 2017 — against a backdrop of a 162% surge in the detainee population, which grew from 2,303 in 2023 to 6,028 at the time of inspection. All seven active California facilities were inspected; an eighth facility (Central Valley Annex) began operations in April 2026. Specific violations documented include "crisis-level" medical staffing (one facility had a single physician for nearly 1,000 detainees), limited food options causing documented nutritional problems, unsanitary bathrooms, dangerously cold temperatures, and pepper spray deployed in confined spaces holding 50 people. Four deaths occurred at Adelanto ICE Processing Center; two at Imperial Regional Detention Facility. Bonta is sponsoring SB 1399 to extend state inspection authority beyond the July 2027 statutory sunset.

This is the fifth in a documented series of California AG inspections that constitute the primary external-audit layer remaining for ICE detention conditions after DHS shut down its own civil-rights oversight offices and defunded legal aid programs beginning January 2025. The serial nature — five consecutive reports with escalating findings — documents the structural-audit-substitution pattern: as federal civil-rights oversight has been dismantled, state AGs have become the de facto accountability mechanism. The six deaths in California alone, combined with the 46 deaths in national ICE custody documented by KFF through March 18, 2026, establish the multi-state fatality pattern at scale. The 162% population surge without proportionate medical staffing increase is the institutional-capacity-failure mechanism that links detention-expansion-as-policy to preventable deaths as consequence.
