An Associated Press investigation (published May 27, 2026 via The Boston Globe; May 26 via Las Vegas Sun) found that at least **10 detainees — all men — have died by suicide in ICE custody since January 2025**, a pace far exceeding the growth in the detainee population. **Seven of those suicides occurred since October 2025 alone — already the most for any fiscal year in the agency's history.** The suicides account for nearly a fifth of the **51 total deaths** in ICE custody since January 2025. **Five of the deaths occurred in facilities run by longtime ICE detention partners CoreCivic and the GEO Group.** Seven of the 10 had no record of violent crimes in the U.S. The investigation links many deaths to substandard, understaffed medical and mental-health care at rapidly expanding warehouse-style facilities.

**Structural significance — suicide cluster as a measurable outcome of the warehouse-detention buildout.** This is a distinct sub-angle from the medical-care/understaffing + transparency-reduction finding at [[2026-05-15--california-ice-detention-deaths-record-high-staffing-crisis-reduced-disclosure]] and the death-count baseline at [[2026-05-07--ice-custody-deaths-reach-17-152-new-detention-facilities-39-states-2026-record-pace]]. The novel finding here is the **suicide-specific pattern**: a record-fiscal-year suicide rate (7 since October), concentrated in the private-contractor (CoreCivic/GEO) facilities, tied to mental-health-care collapse as detention scales into warehouses. The contractor-facility concentration ties the human cost directly to the who-profits financial-spine ([[detention-operator-financial-spine-corecivic-geo-bullish-outlook]]): the same lockup-quota expansion that keeps the operators "bullish" to investors is producing a record suicide rate in their facilities.
