On **June 24, 2026**, Postmaster General **David Steiner** told a Senate committee that, under the proposed rule tied to Trump's election executive orders (EO 14399), the USPS will **withhold mail-ballot delivery from any state that does not provide the federal government with its absentee-ballot request lists.** Five states — **California, Colorado, Connecticut, Maryland, and Washington** — recently passed laws explicitly barring election officials from sharing voter rolls or machine access without a court order, and now face a direct threat that federal mail infrastructure will be turned against their absentee voters before the November 2026 midterms. The proposed rule's comment period closes **July 2**; a federal judge in Massachusetts (Talwani) has allowed legal challenges to proceed.

This is an escalation from the May-29 proposed rule into an explicit on-the-record coercion: federal mail delivery conditioned on state surrender of voter data. It pairs with the SAVE voter-purge track (vacated on the merits 2026-06-22) as the two arms of the same EO-14399 electorate-control architecture — one purges the rolls, the other gates ballot delivery on the rolls. The load-bearing legal question is which EO provision authorizes USPS to condition delivery on voter-roll sharing.
