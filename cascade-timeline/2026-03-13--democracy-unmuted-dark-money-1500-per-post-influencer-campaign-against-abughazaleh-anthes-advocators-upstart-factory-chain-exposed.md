On March 13, 2026 — four days before the IL-09 Democratic primary — MS NOW reporter Brandy Zadrozny published the **complete coordination-chain documentation** of a dark-money paid-influence operation targeting progressive candidate Kat Abughazaleh. The article documents the funder-marketing-agency-creator chain with the email brief, talking points, named per-post payment amount, and on-record statements from a creator who declined the offer and a creator who took it and removed the content when scrutinized.

## The Operation

**Funder (undisclosed)**: An organization called "Democracy Unmuted" that:
- Was registered ~February 27, 2026 (two weeks before the article)
- Has no FEC filings (verified by MS NOW search of FEC records)
- Is not listed in Illinois secretary of state business records
- Operates a website with no "about" section, no contact information, and a non-functional sign-up form
- Per Anthes (the marketing-agency founder), is "not an official org yet" but is funded by "individuals from the IL area who have served in the highest offices and been at top of their game in the media"

**Marketing-agency layer (named)**: Matt Anthes is the named operative running the campaign through two of his agencies:
- **Advocators** (https://advocators.ai) — describes itself as a digital marketing agency focused on politics and advocacy through micro-influencers; lists American Heart Association and the United Nations as past clients (legitimate-cover dual-use vendor pattern)
- **Upstart Factory** — listed by Anthes as the "creative agency partner" executing the campaign; Anthes is also founder of Upstart Factory

Anthes's on-record statement: *"We don't comment on or disclose the identity of our clients. What we can tell you is that all of our dealings and practices are fully compliant with FEC rules and regulations."*

**Creator-payment layer (documented amount)**: $1,500 per Instagram + TikTok post. Email brief with Democracy Unmuted's preferred framing: *"engage voters,"* *"to look past viral personalities and ask real questions about who is running and why."*

**Talking-points layer (verbatim quotes)**:
- Abughazaleh is inexperienced
- Abughazaleh comes from a wealthy family
- Abughazaleh may live with her partner in a different neighborhood
- Abughazaleh is too new to the area to serve
- Brief language: *"Kat's campaign appears designed for attention rather than impact"*

## The Two Documented Creators

**Amanda Informed** (Florida-based, ~100,000 followers): Received the $1,500 offer via email forwarded by Anthes. **Declined.** On-record statement to MS NOW:
> *"The money didn't feel right coming from someone who's not disclosing where the money is coming from. That's not something that I want to be involved in. I want to make sure that it's coming from a source that is not doing nefarious things like interfering with elections."*

**Justin Kralemann** (Missouri-based, ~1 million combined Instagram/TikTok followers): **Took the offer (or content matching the brief).** Posted a video reciting whole sentences from the Democracy Unmuted brief verbatim, including *"to look past viral personalities and ask who is running and why"* (matching a brief-suggested quote). Wore a white hat with "WOKE" emblazoned on the front while mispronouncing Abughazaleh's name. Removed the post immediately upon MS NOW interview request. Stated in email: *"I was not paid for this content"* — but did not return an email asking whether he had been **offered** compensation for the original post.

Kralemann's apology statement: *"Shortly after posting, I realized it did not meet the standards I've set for my platform, so I removed it."*

## Abughazaleh Response

Abughazaleh's statement to MS NOW called the material *"filled with false and defamatory claims about Kat's background and campaign"* and added:

> *"Voters deserve to know who is paying for political influence campaigns like this and whether any campaigns or outside groups are coordinating behind the scenes. We welcome full investigative scrutiny of the entities involved and the sources of their funding."*
