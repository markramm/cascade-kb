Exhibit EFTA00507137, released as part of the 2025 House Oversight Epstein document production, documents that Jeffrey Epstein's personal Mac computer address book contained **direct contact information for the Crown Prince of Dubai** — Sheikh Hamdan bin Mohammed Al Maktoum, Crown Prince of Dubai and son of UAE Vice President / Dubai Ruler Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum. The dating is approximate (the address book was extracted in 2019 but populated over years; reasonable middle estimate is c. 2012 based on adjacent entry timestamps).

The exhibit is structurally significant because it documents **personal-tier access** rather than commercial-tier access. A geopolitical consultant to DP World ([[2010-01-01--epstein-dp-world-geopolitical-consultant-role-begins]]) might have a corporate liaison's office number; a personal address-book entry for the Crown Prince is the artifact of a trust channel beyond the commercial relationship. Combined with the documented 30-DNA-kit shipment to the Al Maktoum family via Emirates Airlines (EFTA00452358), the address-book entry establishes Epstein's positioning at the personal-trust layer of UAE ruling-family access.

Per the Abramson-thesis arc framework ([[epstein-kushner-gulf-sovereign-18-year-arc-abramson-thesis]]), Phase 1 cultivation is precisely this kind of personal-tier relationship-building — the kind that takes 8-18 years to construct and that becomes the *transferable institutional asset* in Phase 2 (when the U.S.-side counterparty rotates). The Crown Prince of Dubai's contact information sitting in Epstein's personal address book in c. 2012 is not a transactional artifact; it is the artifact of a relationship infrastructure that would become operationally available to Kushner and the broader Trump-family-adjacent network 12+ years later.
