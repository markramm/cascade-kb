At a packed **June 23, 2026** county board meeting in Brainerd, Minnesota, **seven of eight residents** demanded that Crow Wing County commissioners withdraw from their ICE agreements: an intergovernmental agreement with the **U.S. Marshals Service** authorizing ICE use of the Crow Wing County Jail (passed unanimously on the consent agenda in August 2025, at **$102/day per detainee**), plus **two 287(g) agreements** — one task-force model and one warrant-service-officer model. The **ACLU** mobilized community attendance. Commissioners have not voted to change the agreements; the pressure remained live with continued coverage on June 25.

This is a characteristic local IGSA/287(g) resistance fight in the detention-pipeline lane: exposure that accumulated quietly (a consent-agenda vote with no substantive debate) now drawing organized public pushback toward a possible formal termination resolution. The task-force 287(g) agreement is the higher-risk instrument (broader ICE cross-designation of local officers). Part of the broader 2026 wave of county-level IGSA/287(g) contests (Bradford County FL, Orange County FL, the Tennessee HB2219 mandate, the NJ/NY 287(g) bans).
