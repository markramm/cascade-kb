ICE awarded a sole-source $25.1 million contract to Bi2 Technologies for 1,570 mobile iris-biometric scanners for field agents and 287(g) partners — roughly five times the prior contract. The award includes access to a private database of approximately 5 million iris records, extending field-deployable biometric identification into the 287(g) deputization network.

The contract is one node in the surveillance-procurement buildout the [[trump-2-surveillance-procurement-chronology-2025-2026]] chronology tracks, and a field-level companion to the consolidated biometric backbone documented in the [[surveillance-mega-database-lineage-connector-1966-2026-dossier-consolidation-cointelpro-to-palantir|surveillance lineage connector]] (HART/IDENT → DHS unified matching engine). The no-bid structure recurs across this buildout — the same non-competitive procurement signature seen in the Palantir awards — and the 5M-record private-database access folds commercial biometric data into ICE's operational reach under the third-party-doctrine framework.
