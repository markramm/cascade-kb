Trump launched a four-day Gulf tour on May 13, 2026, visiting Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and the UAE, accompanied by Elon Musk, Sam Altman, Larry Fink, Nvidia's Jensen Huang, and Alexandr Wang (Scale AI). The financial commitments announced were unprecedented in scale: Saudi Arabia pledged $600B over four years, the UAE pledged $1.4T over a decade, and Qatar added $200B — totaling $2.2T. The deal's central mechanism was Trump's reversal of Biden-era AI chip export restrictions, enabling the UAE and Saudi Arabia to receive advanced Nvidia Blackwell chips. Nvidia immediately announced shipment of 18,000 GB300 chips to Humain, a Saudi sovereign-wealth-backed AI startup; AMD announced a $10B collaboration. The UAE will build what Trump called "the largest AI data center outside the U.S." in Abu Dhabi.

The structural significance is the documented personal-enrichment loop operating through the trip. The UAE's sovereign wealth network (through Sheikh Tahnoon bin Zayed's entities) had previously invested $187M upfront in Trump's World Liberty Financial crypto firm; two months later, UAE received access to advanced U.S. chips; UAE's state-backed stablecoin deal used Trump's USD1 as settlement currency for the $2B MGX-Binance deal. The chip-export reversal to UAE and Saudi Arabia — states where Trump family financial interests are documented — bypassed normal Commerce Department and national security review processes. This event is the clearest single example yet of the foreign-policy-as-personal-enrichment pattern and connects to the crypto-capitalism naming convention in the MAGA Empire series framing.
