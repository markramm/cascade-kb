# ICE/HSI and HHS-OIG Agents Attempt to Enter Legal-Aid Offices Serving Migrant Children

## What Happened

In the week of June 11–13, 2026, agents from **Homeland Security Investigations (HSI, a component of ICE)** accompanied by officials from the **HHS Office of Inspector General** visited the Washington, D.C.-area offices of at least three nonprofit legal-services organizations that represent unaccompanied migrant children: the **Amica Center for Immigrant Rights**, **Ayuda**, and **Kids in Need of Defense (KIND)**. According to ABC News and the Associated Press, the agents sought access to the offices and asked for **financial records tied to the organizations' federal contracts** to provide legal services to unaccompanied children.

Representatives of the groups said the agents **did not present a warrant or subpoena and were denied entry**. Michael Lukens, head of the Amica Center, said agents arrived asking for records related to the legal-services contract. The organizations characterized the visits as an **attempt at intimidation**. The episode follows earlier administration moves to freeze payments to these contractors and to demand sensitive client data.

## Why It Matters

This is **coercion of counsel** — pressure applied not to detainees directly but to the lawyers who represent them, which is the layer that makes judicial review of detention possible at all. The structural target is the **legal-representation infrastructure** for the most procedurally vulnerable population in the detention pipeline: children with no parent present and, often, no right to appointed counsel. Defund the contracts, demand the client files, send armed federal agents to the office door — each step degrades the capacity of children to contest their own removal.

It converges directly with the same-week [[2026-06-10--ice-defies-federal-judge-redetains-77yo-palestinian-omar-louisiana]] event: one move overrides the **judge**, the other degrades the **lawyer**. Both subtract a check on detention-and-removal that exists outside the executive branch. The demand for case files also echoes the transfer-opacity pattern in [[2026-06-01--marshall-project-ice-transfers-disappear-immigrants-attorneys-opacity-architecture]] — control of information about who is detained, and where, is itself a control of accountability.

A parallel ORR/family-separation thread reported the same week (Mother Jones; a federal lawsuit by four children held by the HHS Office of Refugee Resettlement challenging a re-vetting process that strands previously approved sponsors, with ICE agents appearing at sponsor interviews) is the demand-side counterpart of the same campaign against the migrant-children system; it is noted here as context rather than written as a separate thin entry.
