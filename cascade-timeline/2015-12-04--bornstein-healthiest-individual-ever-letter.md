In **December 2015** — confirmed by multiple tier-1 sources as approximately December 4 — Dr. **Harold Bornstein**, then personal physician to Republican presidential candidate **Donald Trump**, released a letter on Trump campaign letterhead declaring: **"If elected, Mr. Trump, I can state unequivocally, will be the healthiest individual ever elected to the presidency."** The letter further stated that Trump's laboratory results were "astonishingly excellent," that "his physical strength and stamina are extraordinary," and that a recent medical examination "showed only positive results." The letter was dated and signed by Bornstein and released publicly by the Trump campaign. At the time of release, Bornstein told reporters he had dashed off the letter in five minutes while Trump's limousine waited outside his office. He described the hyperbolic language as his own, telling CNN in 2016 that he had "picked up his kind of language and then just interpreted it to my own." The letter circulated widely during the 2016 presidential campaign as the primary documentary evidence of Trump's health status, and was treated by news outlets as a physician's professional assessment of his patient.

**Structural significance.** The letter is the originating event in a documented fabrication sequence that was not confirmed until May 2018, when Bornstein admitted to CNN that Trump himself had dictated its content — see [[2018-05-01--bornstein-admits-trump-dictated-physician-letter]]. The functional effect of the letter at time of release was to substitute a subject-authored self-assessment for a physician's independent clinical evaluation, while presenting that self-assessment under a physician's signature and professional authority. This mechanism — subject-controlled disclosure presented as independent expert certification — is the primary substrate for **[[asymmetric-visibility-as-structural-mechanism]]**: the presidential medical disclosure system can be structured by the subject of the disclosure rather than by an independent evaluator, and the public record reflects the subject's framing rather than a clinical finding. The Bornstein 2015 letter is the only instance in the modern presidential-physician disclosure record in which subject-authorship of the letter has been publicly confirmed by the signing physician. It is documented as fabrication in the narrow sense of misrepresented authorship; no claim is made here about the accuracy of the letter's medical content, which remains separately undocumented.
