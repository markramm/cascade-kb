A UCC filing reveals that Tether served as collateral agent for all assets of "Dynasty Trust A" — the trust through which Brandon Lutnick holds majority ownership of Cantor Fitzgerald. The filing exposes that Tether loaned the Lutnick children the money to purchase their father's firm when Howard Lutnick divested his holdings to comply with requirements for his role as Commerce Secretary.

This creates a circular dependency at the heart of U.S. financial and crypto policy: Tether finances the Lutnick family's acquisition of Cantor Fitzgerald, while Howard Lutnick in his capacity as Commerce Secretary shapes cryptocurrency regulation that directly benefits Tether. Cantor Fitzgerald already held a significant custody relationship with Tether's reserves. The arrangement means the Commerce Secretary's children owe their control of a major financial firm to the very entity their father's policies advantage, creating one of the most direct conflicts of interest in modern government-industry relations.
