On **May 1, 2018**, Dr. **Harold Bornstein** gave interviews to **CNN** and **NBC News** in which he confirmed two previously undisclosed facts about his relationship with then-President Trump. First, regarding the December 2015 health letter (see [[2015-12-04--bornstein-healthiest-individual-ever-letter]]): Bornstein told CNN, **"He dictated that whole letter. I didn't write that letter."** He described the process in detail — Trump had read out the language to Bornstein over the phone while Bornstein and his wife were driving across Central Park, with Bornstein offering corrections: **"(Trump) dictated the letter and I would tell him what he couldn't put in there."** This statement directly contradicted Bornstein's 2016 account, in which he had claimed he wrote the letter himself in five minutes as Trump's limousine waited. Second, regarding an incident in **February 2017**: Bornstein told NBC News that on the morning of **February 3, 2017**, two days after he had told the New York Times that Trump took Propecia (a hair-growth drug), Trump's longtime personal bodyguard **Keith Schiller**, Trump Organization lawyer **Alan Garten**, and a third unidentified "large man" arrived unannounced at his Park Avenue office and physically removed Trump's medical records, photographs, and other files. Bornstein described the visit as a **"raid"** and said he felt **"raped, frightened and sad"** by the experience. He stated the men did not present a signed release form from Trump authorizing transfer of the records, which he characterized as a violation of patient privacy law. White House Press Secretary Sarah Huckabee Sanders defended the records transfer as "standard operating procedure for a new president," stating that the White House Medical Unit had taken possession of the president's medical records.

**Structural significance and documented-fabrication status.** The May 2018 Bornstein interviews convert the 2015 letter from a disputed document to a **confirmed fabrication in the specific sense of misrepresented authorship**: the letter was signed by a physician but authored by the patient whose health the letter purported to assess. This is documented not as allegation but as the signing physician's own on-record admission, corroborated by CNN and NBC News reporting. The admission establishes what the asymmetric-visibility analysis requires: a documented instance — not an inferred pattern — in which presidential medical disclosure was structured by the subject of that disclosure rather than by an independent evaluator, and in which the independent-evaluator framing was maintained publicly for more than two years. The simultaneous disclosure of the February 2017 records seizure adds a second documented mechanism: the removal of Bornstein's copy of Trump's medical records by agents acting on Trump's behalf, without documented legal authorization, shortly after Bornstein disclosed medical information (the Propecia prescription) without prior Trump approval. The sequencing — disclosure by physician, records seizure two days later, and physician's public silence until 2018 — is documented in the public record as described here. The inference that the records seizure was intended to prevent further independent disclosure is not established in the documentary record and is not claimed here; the temporal sequence and Bornstein's contemporaneous characterization are documented as stated. For no-extrapolation discipline: no claim is made from this substrate that other presidential physician letters have been fabricated; the Bornstein 2015 letter is the only instance with confirmed physician admission.
