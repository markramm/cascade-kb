On or about March 11, 2026, **Deutsche Bank AG**'s asset-management subsidiary — operating through DWS Group and its real-estate arm **RREEF America** (the former Rosenberg Real Estate Equity Funds, acquired by Deutsche Bank in 2002) — sold a warehouse in Salt Lake City, Utah to the U.S. Department of Homeland Security for **$145.4 million**. The sale represented a **~49% markup** over the property's assessed valuation. The Salt Lake City transaction is the **single largest** of the eleven documented WEXMAC-TITUS warehouse acquisitions by dollar amount, and closes roughly six weeks later than the initial January 2026 cluster of Surprise/Williamsport/Tremont/Social Circle.

## Transaction Details

- **Property**: 6020 W 300 S, Salt Lake City, UT (Mountain View Industrial Park, Gardner Logistics Center) — 833,280 sq ft on 47 acres (two parcels)
- **Seller entity**: **RREEF CPIF 6020 W 300 S LLC** (Delaware LLC; 222 S. Riverside Plaza, 26th Floor, Chicago IL 60606); property-holding subsidiary of **RREEF Core Plus Industrial Fund L.P.** (CPIF; ~$2.37B private fund, now liquidated), managed by RREEF America L.L.C. → DWS Group GmbH & Co. KGaA → Deutsche Bank AG (~75–80% owner)
- **Purchase price**: $145.44 million ($145.4M on deed for parcel 1; $10 nominal on April 13, 2026 deed for parcel 2)
- **2025 assessed value**: $97.37M (parcel 1); combined market value ~$114M (both parcels)
- **Markup over assessment**: ~49% (parcel 1); ~28% over combined market value
- **Pre-sale appraisal**: Reported at ~$130M by NYT/DNYUZ (April 7, 2026) — unconfirmed by primary source
- **RREEF 2022 acquisition**: RREEF CPIF purchased 6020 W 300 S in 2022; cost basis not publicly documented
- **Procurement vehicle**: WEXMAC-TITUS (Navy WEXMAC 2.1 multiple-award vehicle, TITUS designation)
- **Planned capacity**: 7,500 detention beds (reported); not yet confirmed by ICE
- **Retrofit contractor**: Not publicly identified as of May 2026

## The Forst Legacy-Pension Conflict

**Edward Forst**, the GSA Administrator during the Salt Lake City transaction, holds a **Deutsche Bank defined-benefit pension plan** dating to his September 1, 1982 start date at **Bankers Trust**. Bankers Trust was acquired by Deutsche Bank in June 1999; the pension obligations transferred. Per his ProPublica disclosure:

- Estimated pension payment: **$1,644/month at age 65**
- Forst's spouse also holds a Deutsche Bank defined-benefit plan (from the same Bankers Trust lineage)
- Forst did not work at Deutsche Bank directly, but maintains an ongoing financial relationship through the inherited pension

The Salt Lake City transaction is therefore the **second direct-seller conflict** on Forst's GSA watch: his Goldman Sachs alumni and retained PE exposure ties to the Roxbury, NJ transaction ([[2026-02--dhs-purchases-goldman-dalfen-roxbury-nj-warehouse]]); his Deutsche Bank legacy pension ties to Salt Lake City. Combined former-employer proceeds from both transactions: **$274.7 million**.

## The Stephen Feinberg Conflict

**Stephen Feinberg** (Deputy Secretary of Defense) holds **multiple Deutsche Bank AG assets and Deutsche Bank commercial mortgage-backed securities** per his ProPublica disclosure. DOD administers WEXMAC-TITUS. Feinberg is therefore a ranking official at the department that runs the contracting vehicle through which Deutsche Bank's DWS/RREEF subsidiary executed a $145.4M sale to DHS.

Feinberg is separately documented as a private equity principal (founder of Cerberus Capital Management) — the intersection of his personal PE portfolio with Deutsche Bank-structured instruments is an open research question.

## The Atkins Triple Exposure

**Paul Atkins** (SEC Chairman, $324M net worth) holds financial ties to **three** of the eleven WEXMAC-TITUS warehouse sellers:

- **Goldman Sachs compensation** (Roxbury NJ seller, Williamsport MD lender)
- **Carlyle Finance LLC bonds** (Oakwood GA seller)
- **Deutsche Bank AG compensation** (Salt Lake City UT seller)

As SEC Chairman, Atkins is the top securities regulator. The Goldman, Carlyle, and Deutsche Bank banking subsidiaries and public-equity reporting fall under SEC jurisdiction in various respects. Atkins' triple exposure to three of the eleven warehouse sellers — while overseeing the securities regulator that would receive any disclosure-related complaints about the transactions — is the most concentrated single-individual conflict in the detention-industrial conflict map.

## The Gould Regulatory-Regulated Relationship

**Jonathan Gould** (Comptroller of the Currency) regulates national banks including Deutsche Bank's U.S. subsidiaries. Gould separately holds Blue Owl Real Estate Net Lease Property Fund positions (Blue Owl executed Tremont) and has former Goldman Sachs compensation. Gould's OCC portfolio therefore spans the banking-subsidiary regulators for three of the eleven warehouse sellers while his personal financial exposure ties to Blue Owl's and Goldman's transactions.

## The Deutsche Bank–Epstein Context

The detention-industrial KB's Forst profile notes the **Deutsche Bank / Epstein connection**: Deutsche Bank maintained 40 Epstein accounts consolidated at $319 million in assets and was fined $225 million by New York regulators for the relationship. The same institution that maintained the Epstein accounts is now a direct WEXMAC-TITUS seller at the largest dollar amount in the program. The administration officials with Deutsche Bank exposure (Forst pension, Feinberg assets, Atkins compensation, Gould regulatory relationship) therefore sit in financial relation to an institution with a regulatory and moral record already under scrutiny.

## The Foreign-Bank-Seller Dimension

Deutsche Bank AG is a German-headquartered global banking institution (Frankfurt). The Salt Lake City transaction is therefore the **second foreign-parent warehouse-seller case** in the WEXMAC-TITUS eleven-transaction cluster (after Rockefeller Group / Mitsubishi Estate at Surprise AZ — see [[2026-01-23--dhs-purchases-rockefeller-surprise-az-warehouse]]). Combined foreign-parent transaction total: **$215.4 million** ($70M Surprise + $145.4M SLC).

The third foreign-origin seller in the cluster is PNK / Sharkov at Social Circle ($128.5M) — a Russian-founded firm whose corporate severance from Russia in 2023 is documented but whose owner maintains Moscow residence (see [[sharkov-andrey]]). Combined foreign-origin-seller total across the WEXMAC-TITUS program: **$343.9 million**, or ~32% of the $1.074 billion program total to date.

## Significance

The Salt Lake City transaction closes the initial phase of the WEXMAC-TITUS buildout at the largest single dollar amount. The conflict architecture is the most multi-point of any single transaction:

1. **Forst (GSA)** — legacy pension from seller's 1999-acquired predecessor
2. **Feinberg (DOD Deputy Sec)** — Deutsche Bank AG assets, DOD administers procurement vehicle
3. **Atkins (SEC Chairman)** — Deutsche Bank compensation (plus Goldman and Carlyle exposure)
4. **Gould (OCC)** — regulator of Deutsche Bank's U.S. banking subsidiaries

Four named administration officials, each in a different oversight or regulatory relation to the selling institution, each with documented personal financial exposure. No single conflict is individually large enough to stop a transaction, but the cumulative pattern means no administration official with cross-cutting authority over Deutsche Bank was in a position to raise the conflict issue without implicating their own exposure.
