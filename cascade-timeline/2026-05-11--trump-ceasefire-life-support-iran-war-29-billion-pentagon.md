Iran's parliament speaker responded that the military stands ready to "teach a lesson." Same day, Pentagon comptroller Jules Hurst informed House lawmakers that the U.S. war has now cost approximately $29 billion, revised up from a $25 billion late-April estimate. The war is in its 10th week; Brent crude trades at $105/barrel (up 44% from pre-war); U.S. gas at $4.06/gallon; April inflation hit 3.8%, the highest in three years.
