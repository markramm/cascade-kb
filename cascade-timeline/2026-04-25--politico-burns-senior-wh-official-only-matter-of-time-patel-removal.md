Politico White House correspondent Dasha Burns reported on April 25 that a senior White House official, granted anonymity, told her: "It's only a matter of time" regarding FBI Director Kash Patel's tenure. The official cited the volume of negative stories about Patel as "not a good look for a Cabinet secretary" and said Trump was "fed up with the level of distraction." White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt issued an on-record statement that Patel remains "a critical player on the administration's law and order team."

Senior White House officials do not tell political reporters "it's only a matter of time" by accident. The leak — coming eight days after The Atlantic's reporting (April 17), five days after Patel's $250M lawsuit (April 20), four days after the SPLC indictment (April 21), and one day after the Intercept surfaced Patel's 2005 personnel-file alcohol-arrest disclosure (April 24) — represents administration preparation for an exit ramp. The pattern fits prior cabinet shuffles managed via anonymous-sourced Politico placements (e.g., the Hegseth-Navy Secretary signal). Whether Patel is removed in days or weeks, the architectural critique holds: the Qatar bilateral MOU is in force, the gutted Public Integrity Section remains gutted, the fired Iran counterintelligence unit cannot be quickly reconstituted, and the SPLC indictment proceeds.
