In March 2022, then-Senate candidate JD Vance appeared on The Eric Metaxas Show — a radio and podcast platform broadcast on over 300 stations nationally and distributed through Trinity Broadcasting Network (TBN), anchored in the evangelical and charismatic Christian audience that overlaps significantly with Pentecostal and NAR networks. The interview covered the Ohio Senate race through the framework of a Christian cultural war, with Metaxas framing the Democratic party and the academic left as mounting "attempts to ban God from the public square." Vance framed conservative voters as spiritually and politically awake: "Our voters are very awake to what's going on in the country." The interview was subsequently rebroadcast in July 2024 as an "encore" when Vance was named Trump's VP pick, confirming its perceived significance as a marker of Vance's evangelical-adjacent alignment.

## What Happened / Key Facts

The Eric Metaxas Show is among the most prominent broadcast platforms reaching evangelical and charismatic Christian audiences in the United States. Metaxas — author of biographies of Dietrich Bonhoeffer, William Wilberforce, and Martin Luther — occupies a hybrid space between Reformed evangelical intellectual culture and the charismatic-Pentecostal broadcast ecosystem. He is distributed on TBN (Trinity Broadcasting Network, the flagship Pentecostal/charismatic television network) and reaches an audience that spans evangelical, Pentecostal, and NAR-adjacent Christian communities. His 2020 election denialism and his alignment with Donald Trump (he publicly predicted Trump's victory would be "a miracle from God") place him squarely in the charismatic-nationalist mobilization circuit.

Metaxas framed the interview's stakes in explicitly theological terms: the political contest was framed as a fight against "the ideas of the Marxist left — because that's what it is now, it's the Marxist left, these are fundamentally atheistic, fundamentally anti-American ideas" that have "taken over the academy." Vance aligned with this framework, describing Ohio Republican voters as "very awake to what's going on in the country" and promising that conservatives would eventually have "a real class of fighters in Washington" who would "take back the country."

The interview also addressed:
- "Attempts to ban God from the public square" — Metaxas's framing, which Vance engaged without distancing
- The "grace shown to billionaire playboy Donald Trump by the evangelical Christian community" — framing Trump's evangelical support in theological terms of forgiveness
- Vance's military service in Iraq and his memoir *Hillbilly Elegy* as markers of authentic working-class Christian identity
- Election security concerns, which Metaxas had been vocally promoting in line with the 2020 election-denial movement

The interview was conducted before the June 2022 Roe v. Wade overturn (Dobbs v. Jackson). It was rebroadcast in July 2024 after Trump named Vance his VP pick, an "encore" presentation that the Metaxas Show marketed as a "look back" at its earlier access to the VP nominee.

## Why This Event Matters

Eric Metaxas occupies a structurally significant position in the Pentecostal/NAR-adjacent Christian nationalist ecosystem. His show's distribution through TBN — the flagship charismatic/Pentecostal broadcast network — places it within the same audience infrastructure as Lance Wallnau's Courage Tour and TPUSA Faith's Faith Forward Pastors' Summit. Vance's 2022 appearance on the Metaxas Show is an early documented engagement with this broadcast audience, preceding by two years the more explicit Courage Tour appearance (September 28, 2024) documented in [[2024-09-28--vance-wallnau-courage-tour]].

The "attempts to ban God from the public square" framing is specifically calibrated for the Pentecostal/evangelical broadcast audience: it invokes religious persecution as a frame for political mobilization, speaking the spiritual-warfare vocabulary (the godless left vs. faithful America) that characterizes NAR-adjacent political theology. Vance's willingness to sit with Metaxas and engage this framing without distancing — while simultaneously appearing at Catholic postliberal conferences (Steubenville, October 2022) using entirely different intellectual vocabulary — is the documentary evidence of substrate-specific vocabulary calibration across the three tracks.

The 2024 encore rebroadcast confirms the Metaxas show's perception of the interview's ongoing value as a coalition-alignment marker for its evangelical-charismatic audience.

## Broader Context

Eric Metaxas's alignment with the Christian nationalist circuit accelerated significantly after 2020. His 2021 book *Is Atheism Dead?* and his sustained election-denial advocacy placed him within the most aggressive wing of evangelical political mobilization. By March 2022, when Vance appeared, Metaxas was already a prominent figure in the overlap between evangelical media, election-denial, and the MAGA movement.

Metaxas later publicly celebrated Vance's VP nomination in July 2024: "Thrilled to say that JD Vance is EXACTLY the right choice for VP." The March 2022 interview predates that endorsement by 28 months, establishing the relationship as pre-VP and campaign-era rather than post-nomination convenience.

The Metaxas platform's TBN distribution connects it directly to the Pentecostal broadcast infrastructure that Wallnau, Paula White, and other NAR-adjacent figures use to reach their constituencies. Vance's appearance on Metaxas is thus less a "charismatic venue" in the strictest sense than a cross-audience-infrastructure appearance that reached Pentecostal and evangelical audiences simultaneously through the same broadcast network.
