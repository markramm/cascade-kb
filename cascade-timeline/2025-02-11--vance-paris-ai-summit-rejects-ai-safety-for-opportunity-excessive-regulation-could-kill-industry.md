On February 11, 2025, Vice President JD Vance delivered the Trump administration's signature AI-policy address at the **Artificial Intelligence Action Summit in Paris**, reframing the international AI agenda from *safety* to *opportunity* and *dominance*. The speech's defining line: **"I'm not here this morning to talk about AI safety, which was the title of the conference a couple of years ago. I'm here to talk about AI opportunity."** Vance warned that **"excessive regulation of the AI sector could kill a transformative industry just as it's taking off,"** and declared, **"The United States of America is the leader in AI, and our administration plans to keep it that way."**

Vance laid out **four administration priorities** for AI: (1) American AI technology must remain **"the gold standard worldwide"**; (2) avoid **"excessive regulation"**; (3) **"AI must remain free from ideological bias"** and never restrict "our citizens' right to free speech"; (4) a **"pro-worker growth path"** promising "higher wages, better benefits." The address was a pointed rejection of European-style precautionary AI regulation and followed Trump's rescission (January 2025) of President Biden's October 2023 AI executive order — which had emphasized safety, oversight, and anti-discrimination — in favor of an "AI dominance" framing (White House transcript via the American Presidency Project; CNN; PBS).
