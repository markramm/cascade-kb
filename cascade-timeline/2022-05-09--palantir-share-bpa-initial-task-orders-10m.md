Five days after announcing the five-year, $90 million [[share-bpa-palantir|HHS SHARE BPA]] with Palantir Technologies, the HHS Program Support Center issued the first two task orders under the agreement — combined value $10.4 million.

**Task order 75P00122F37001** ($7,807,741, 11-month period) covered Palantir Foundry **enterprise licenses** for HHS sub-agencies. **Task order 75P00122F37002** ($2,581,421, 11-month period) covered **HHS CARES Act lanes and outcome data integration support** — reporting infrastructure for HHS's pandemic-response financial assistance programs.

Both task orders were issued by the Office of the Assistant Secretary for Administration (ASA) Program Support Center Acquisition Management Services, and both received only a single bidder — the structural non-competition pattern that would continue through subsequent SHARE task orders ([[2024-09-20--palantir-acf-ucbc-12m-share-bpa-task-order]], [[2025-08-04--palantir-iccm-19m-share-bpa-task-order]]).

These two initial task orders established the operational pattern: ASA-awarded, Foundry-based, sole-bidder, funding Palantir's cross-agency platform footprint rather than any specific agency's use case. The enterprise-license task order in particular made Foundry a department-wide fixture rather than a CDC-only or NIH-only tool — the architectural prerequisite for the later [[2025-02-13--clark-minor-appointed-hhs-cto-from-palantir|Clark Minor-era expansion]] to all HHS sub-agencies.
