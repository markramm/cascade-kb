As of early May 2026, the Trump administration has pushed multiple states to submit entire voter rolls to DHS's SAVE (Systematic Alien Verification for Entitlements) database to flag potential non-citizen registrants ahead of the 2026 midterms. At least one state board voted to send its entire voter list to DHS for SAVE comparison in April 2026. Ohio and Texas are conducting voter purges that election lawyers argue potentially violate the National Voter Registration Act's 90-day pre-election prohibition on systematic purges. An Idaho review using SAVE initially flagged 760 potential non-citizen voters; law enforcement referrals after review fell to approximately 36 — a 95% false-positive rate on naturalized citizens whose citizenship records predate SAVE's integration with Social Security Administration data. CNN reports Trump and Republican officials are "testing the legal precedent" by pushing purges as close to Election Day as possible. The SAVE system has been expanded beyond its original purpose, consolidated with SSA data, and applied to voter rolls without documented judicial authorization for that specific use.

The SAVE purge blitz and the SCOTUS Callais ruling (documented in `2026-05-04--scotus-callais-vra-section-2-immediate-effect-order-overrides-100k-ballots`) are operating simultaneously: federal courts have removed the structural protection for majority-minority districts while the executive branch is removing minority voters from the rolls — the 95% false-positive rate on naturalized citizens makes the disparate racial impact explicit. These are not parallel tracks; they are mutually reinforcing mechanisms for the 2026 midterm cycle. This documents the election-systems capture pattern fully active in-cycle, distinct from the 2024-cycle documented events. The SAVE Act (which passed the House in February 2026 and was pending Senate action as of May 2026) would further codify this mechanism — the executive branch is already operating under the Act's logic before it has become law.
