On May 26, 2026, Texas Attorney General **Ken Paxton defeated four-term incumbent Sen. John Cornyn in the Republican Senate primary runoff**, after President Trump endorsed Paxton the prior week (during early voting). The race cost Republicans more than $100 million. Paxton advances to face Democratic nominee state Rep. James Talarico in November. Trump's endorsement statement is the documented framing: Cornyn "was not supportive of me when times were tough," while Paxton is "a Fighter, and knows how to WIN… Our Country needs Fighters, and also **Loyalty to the Cause of Greatness.**" Paxton prevailed despite years of corruption allegations — a 2023 state impeachment, securities-fraud charges, and a whistleblower-retaliation settlement (NPR, CNBC, Time).

**Structural significance — loyalty-enforcement against in-party oversight dissent.** This is electoral politics on its surface (a president endorsing a primary challenger is ordinary), but the capture-relevant element is the *mechanism*: it is a named, dated instance of the same loyalty-over-institutionalism purge documented at [[2026-05-18--hegseth-campaigns-against-massie-ky04-breaking-pentagon-neutrality-hatch-act]] — Trump-2 removing Republicans deemed "insufficiently supportive," with the explicit "Loyalty to the Cause" criterion stated openly. The sharper structural point is the *direction of selection*: a corruption-allegation-laden figure (Paxton) elevated *over* an institutionalist (Cornyn), with the corruption non-disqualifying and loyalty the operative test — consistent with the accountability-elimination / forgiveness-over-accountability frame (corruption is not a liability; disloyalty is). Importance held at 6: a pattern-instance of in-party-dissent-purge, not a constitutional-crisis event.
