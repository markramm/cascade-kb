# "Progressive Champions PAC" Exposed as GOP Front; Polymarket Ends Sponsorship of Election-Fraud Posts

## What Happened

Two linked election-capture findings by **Popular Information** (Judd Legum) in early-to-mid June 2026:

**1. Astroturf super PAC.** A new super PAC calling itself **Progressive Champions PAC** — whose website claims a mission to "elect bold, progressive candidates up and down the ballot" — booked **roughly $400,000 in broadcast TV ads attacking Cait Conley, a Democratic candidate in New York's 17th Congressional District** (per FCC records). Popular Information traced its financial plumbing: both Progressive Champions PAC and a related entity, **Real Change PAC**, list **Bryant Bank**, a small Alabama bank, as their bank of record — and Bryant Bank's most prominent customer is **Rep. Gary Palmer (R-AL)**. The "progressive" PAC, in other words, appears to be a **Republican-operated front** running ads to damage a Democrat from the left.

**2. Prediction-market-sponsored fraud narrative.** Popular Information reported that **Polymarket** — the online prediction market that brands itself a "truth machine" — was **paying a network of at least 16 online influencers** to post on X content claiming, without evidence, that the **Los Angeles mayoral election was "rigged"** against a Republican reality-TV candidate. After the reporting gained traction, **Polymarket ended the sponsorships**, saying the posts violated its marketing guidelines barring affiliates from spreading misleading or false information. NPR reported a parallel Kalshi/Polymarket crackdown on paid influencers pushing California election-fraud claims.

## Why It Matters

This is **election-capture by information laundering** — two variants of the same move: disguise the source so the message is trusted.

- The astroturf PAC **counterfeits the messenger's identity** (a GOP operation wearing a "progressive" label) to split or suppress the Democratic coalition in a targeted House race. The capture is of *authenticity* — voters cannot evaluate a message whose true sponsor is hidden.
- The Polymarket sponsorship **monetizes the manufacture of a fraud narrative**, paying influencers to seed "rigged election" claims that have no evidentiary basis — pre-loading the delegitimization story the corpus has flagged as the right's "election fraud" cry being rehearsed in the 2026 primaries ahead of the midterms.

Both feed the same downstream capability: a contested-results playbook in which the *appearance* of grassroots progressive opposition and the *appearance* of organic fraud allegations are purchased. It converges with the structural-vote-dilution thread already in the timeline — the VRA gutting and Southern redistricting ([[2026-06-12--vra-gutted-southern-states-race-to-redraw-maps-eliminating-majority-black-districts]]) reshape *who can win*; the astroturf-and-fraud-narrative apparatus reshapes *whether a result will be believed*. Convergence, not coordination.

The Polymarket reversal is the one accountability win in the cluster: independent journalism (Popular Information) forced the platform to drop the sponsorships within days — a documented instance of the discovery-via-independent-reporting → correction loop.

## Verified Absences / Attribution

- The Bryant Bank / Gary Palmer connection is Popular Information's documented inference from bank-of-record filings; this entry attributes the "GOP front" characterization to that reporting rather than asserting a proven coordination/control relationship between Palmer and the PAC.
- The full list of the 16 influencers and the exact Polymarket payment amounts were not captured here.
