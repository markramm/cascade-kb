President Trump signed an executive order on June 2, 2026 titled "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security," directing frontier AI model developers to voluntarily provide the federal government up to 30 days of pre-release access before sharing with "trusted partners." The order also mandates rapid deployment of AI-enabled cybersecurity tools across federal agencies, establishes a classified benchmarking process to designate "covered frontier model" thresholds, and creates a Treasury-led AI Cybersecurity Clearinghouse. Crucially, it explicitly bans mandatory governmental licensing or preclearance for AI models.

Coming alongside Trump's December 2025 EO 14365 preempting state AI laws and a DOJ AI Litigation Task Force targeting state AI statutes, the June order completes the administration's capture of AI governance: federal primacy, voluntary safety, national-security framing. The "voluntary" 30-day pre-deployment window with "trusted partners" mirrors export-control logic and risks becoming a de facto procurement condition — a surveillance touchpoint over frontier models before public release that requires no Congressional authorization.
