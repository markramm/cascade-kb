# SBC Advances Women-Pastor Ban and Elects Abuse-Crisis Skeptic Willy Rice President

## What Happened

At its June 2026 Annual Meeting, the **Southern Baptist Convention** — the largest Protestant denomination in the United States — took two linked actions:

1. **Women-pastor ban advanced.** Messengers voted **6,028 to 2,026** (roughly 3-to-1, well over the required two-thirds) to **advance a constitutional amendment formally barring churches with women pastors** from the SBC. As a constitutional amendment, it requires a second two-thirds vote at the 2027 annual meeting to take effect.

2. **Willy Rice elected president.** Delegates elected **Willy Rice**, a Clearwater, Florida pastor, as SBC president with about **58% of the vote** over South Carolina pastor **Josh Powell**. Rice drew support from advocacy groups including the **Center for Baptist Leadership**, which argued SBC leadership had gone "woke" on race, gender, and immigration. Per Religion News Service and Word&Way, Rice has characterized the SBC's **sexual-abuse crisis as more hoax than reality** and argued denominational leaders had "followed the culture more than the Bible." Rice supported the women-pastor amendment, as did Powell and outgoing president Clint Pressley.
