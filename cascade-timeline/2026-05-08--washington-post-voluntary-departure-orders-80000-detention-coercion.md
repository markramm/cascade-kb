A Washington Post investigation published May 8, 2026 found that immigration judges have issued more than 80,000 voluntary-departure orders since Trump's January 2025 inauguration, with more than 70 percent of those granted such orders held in immigration detention at the time of the request — a near-inversion of the Biden-era pattern, when most voluntary departures came from non-detained respondents. Monthly voluntary departures rose from roughly 750 during the latter half of the Biden administration to more than 6,000 after the July 2025 enforcement-raid escalation, and reached 9,000 in March 2026 alone. The reporting documents that the surge does not reflect a free choice to leave: detention conditions, the collapse of asylum win-rates under the restructured immigration courts, and the indefinite duration of pre-hearing custody have produced a coerced-departure system functioning at industrial scale.

This is the underside of the same numerator the administration cites as "self-deportation" success. It connects directly to the March 18 internal DHS document showing "Project Homecoming" enrollments far below administration claims, to the December 2025 DHS year-end report combining voluntary departures with deportations to inflate totals, to the November 2025 ICE in-custody-death pattern (one death every six days through May 2026, now at 17), and to the FY2026 detention data showing 73 percent of those booked into ICE custody have no criminal conviction. The structural reading: the administration has built a detention pipeline whose purpose is not adjudication but exhaustion. People with valid asylum claims abandon them not because they lack merit, but because the cost of asserting them is prolonged confinement in facilities with documented lethal conditions. That is removal by attrition under color of voluntary process.
