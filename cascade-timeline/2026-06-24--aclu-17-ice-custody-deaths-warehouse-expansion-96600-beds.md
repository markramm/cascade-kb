The ACLU documented 17 deaths in ICE custody in 2026 — roughly one every six days — as ICE actively pursues conversion of more than 20 commercial warehouses that would push total detention capacity to 96,600 beds. The ACLU report links the spiking death rate directly to rapid facility expansion and declining oversight at newly opened sites.

This is the harm/oversight counterpart to the DHS-program side of the same buildout: the "Detention Reengineering Initiative" targeting 100,000 beds via 24 warehouse-converted facilities ([[2026-06-23--ice-detention-reengineering-initiative-100k-warehouse-network]]), the $38B mega-center expansion ([[2026-03-12--ice-detention-expansion-38-billion-mega-centers]]), and the national resistance day of action ([[2026-04-25--communities-not-cages-national-day-of-action-ice-warehouse-detention]]). Where the program entries document the capacity target, this entry documents the cost — the death rate is the down-ratchet's terminal measurement, the same accountability-thinning the [[detention-as-labor-extraction-thirteenth-amendment-exception-as-operator-profit-margin-per-bed-day-occupancy-unpaid-detainee-labor-mortality-pressure-2026|detention-as-labor-extraction]] theme names as occupancy-pressure → mortality.
