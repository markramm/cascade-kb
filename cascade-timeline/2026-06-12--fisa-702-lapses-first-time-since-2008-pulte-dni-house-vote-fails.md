The House failed to pass a short-term FISA Section 702 extension on June 11, 2026, 198-218, allowing the foreign surveillance authority to lapse at midnight — the first time Section 702 has expired since it was enacted via the FISA Amendments Act of 2008. The proximate cause: House Democrats refused to vote for any extension until Trump reversed his decision to install Bill Pulte — director of the Federal Housing Finance Agency with no national security or intelligence experience — as acting Director of National Intelligence, replacing Tulsi Gabbard (effective June 19). The same day the program lapsed (June 12), Trump announced he would nominate Jay Clayton (Manhattan U.S. Attorney, former SEC chair) as permanent DNI, with a Senate confirmation hearing scheduled for June 17. Pulte remains in an acting capacity until Clayton is confirmed.

Two distinct structural reads. **First: the succession/loyalty-capture vector.** Pulte's installation as acting DNI — giving him supervisory authority over all 18 intelligence community components (CIA, NSA, DIA, and the rest) — is the most extreme instance to date of the loyalty-over-expertise succession pattern this timeline tracks. Pulte had been the instrument of Trump's political retribution campaign via FHFA mortgage-data searches against Democratic critics ([[2025-08-15--pulte-weaponizes-mortgage-data-against-fed-governor]], [[2025-08-20--pulte-mortgage-fraud-campaign-against-democrats]], [[2025-12-04--gao-investigates-pulte-weaponized-criminal-referrals]]); installing him atop the intelligence community is the "capture the clearinghouse" move — the same escalation arc that ran through Ratcliffe's retraction of 19 CIA intelligence products ([[2026-02-20--cia-ratcliffe-retracts-19-intelligence-products-political-independence]]) and Gabbard's firing of career analysts who contradicted White House positions ([[2025-05-14--gabbard-fires-venezuela-analysts]]). **Second: surveillance paradox as counter-pattern.** Democrats using 702's expiration as leverage is structurally unusual — it is the first time a civil-liberties-adjacent position (opposing the loyalty-capture of DNI) has accidentally produced a resistance outcome against surveillance authority. This is counter-data against the usual "Democrats acquiesce on surveillance" pattern documented across the FISA arc from [[1978-10-25--fisa-executive-power-statute-bargain]] through [[2008-07-09--fisa-amendments-act-passed-telecom-immunity]] to [[2026-04-29--congress-passes-fisa-702-45-day-extension-short-term]]. That April 45-day extension had bought Congress until mid-June specifically for the Patel-firing bargaining dynamics ([[2026-04-28--bloomberg-government-fire-patel-fisa-reauth-bargaining-chip]]); the substitution of Pulte-appointment leverage for Patel-firing leverage is the same Democrats-withhold-702-votes tactic, now applied to a DNI appointment rather than an FBI director. The program's lapse carries immediate operational consequences for NSA/FBI counterterrorism surveillance — the administration's own intelligence agencies lose a primary foreign-collection tool while the acting head of those agencies is a political-retribution operative.
