Per Exhibit EFTA01617920 from the 2025 House Oversight Epstein document production, in June 2019 — immediately after George Nader's arrest on federal child pornography charges — Steve Bannon and Jeffrey Epstein exchanged messages discussing the arrest in real time. The recovered exchange:

- **Bannon**: "MBZ must have coughed him up"
- **Epstein**: "He was duped."

The exchange is structurally load-bearing for the Phase 1 thesis ([[epstein-kushner-gulf-sovereign-18-year-arc-abramson-thesis]]) because it materially confirms three claims that would otherwise rest on synthesis-level inference:

1. **Operational MBZ awareness of the Nader channel**: Bannon's "MBZ must have coughed him up" presupposes that MBZ controlled — or could plausibly choose to release — Nader's exposure to U.S. federal law enforcement. This is not a claim about MBZ being aware of Nader as a public figure; it is a claim that MBZ had operational control over the U.S.-facing channel Nader represented.

2. **Bannon-Epstein direct coordination on Gulf-principal politics**: Epstein and Bannon were in close enough operational coordination to discuss MBZ's strategic decisions in real time, in shorthand, without explanation. This is the communication pattern of operational partners, not adjacent figures.

3. **Epstein's continued centrality five weeks before his arrest**: Epstein was arrested July 6, 2019 — approximately five weeks after this exchange. The exchange establishes that Epstein was still functioning as an operational node in the Gulf-coordination network at that point, not as a retired or peripheral figure.

The exchange is also evidence for the broader "intelligence-service awareness on multiple sides" reading that Abramson advances. Bannon's framing — "MBZ must have coughed him up" — assumes a shared understanding between Bannon and Epstein that MBZ-aligned UAE intelligence services routinely make such operational-disposability decisions about U.S.-facing assets. This is consistent with the operational structure of the Phase 1 cultivation network as Abramson reads it: a multi-state intelligence environment in which the U.S.-side and Gulf-side principals were aware of each other's operational dispositions.
