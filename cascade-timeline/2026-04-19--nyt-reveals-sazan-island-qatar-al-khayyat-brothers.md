On **April 19, 2026**, the **New York Times** published an investigation revealing that **"Sazan Operations"** — the Albanian corporate entity behind Jared Kushner and Ivanka Trump's **€1.4 billion luxury resort project** on Sazan Island — is **100% owned by Qatari billionaires Ramez and Mohamad Al-Khayyat**. **Ramez Al-Khayyat confirmed the Kushner partnership** directly to the NYT. The revelation closes a significant information gap: the Sazan Island project had been publicly framed as a Kushner/Affinity Partners venture without disclosure of the specific Qatari capital source behind its operating entity.

The Sazan project has been under development since 2024 and was cleared by the Albanian government's **Strategic Investment Committee** despite environmental opposition from **41 environmental organizations in 28 countries**. The total projected investment is **€1.4 billion**, and the project will convert the **1,400-acre uninhabited island** into a luxury eco-resort featuring the **Aman brand**.

## The Al-Khayyat Brothers

**Ramez and Mohamad Al-Khayyat** are Qatar-based billionaires with business interests spanning:

- Real estate development across the Gulf and Europe
- Hospitality and luxury hotels
- Holding-company structures registered in multiple offshore jurisdictions
- Documented relationships with Qatari government officials and royal family

The Al-Khayyat brothers' corporate structure behind "Sazan Operations" involves, per the BIRN (Balkan Investigative Reporting Network) and Citizens.al reporting, **a complex network of intermediate companies** that obscured the underlying Qatari ownership until the April 2026 NYT investigation. The specific corporate chain has been partially documented by Albanian investigative press but not fully mapped.

Ramez Al-Khayyat **confirmed the Kushner partnership to the New York Times** — a statement that converts the relationship from speculative to documented.

## The Project Structure

- **Location**: Sazan Island, 1,400 acres, uninhabited, off the coast of Vlorë, Albania
- **Projected investment**: **€1.4 billion**
- **Development type**: Luxury eco-resort, Aman brand
- **Employment projection**: 1,000 jobs during development and operation
- **Operating entity**: "Sazan Operations" (100% Al-Khayyat brothers)
- **Kushner role**: Affinity Partners partnership
- **Ivanka Trump role**: Active involvement — visited Vlorë, engaged with Albanian PM Edi Rama
- **Albanian government**: Approved via Strategic Investment Committee
- **Negotiations**: 10 rounds held by December 2025
- **Status**: Cleared, but no feasibility study or final plan submitted as of April 2026

## The Environmental Context

**41 environmental organizations from 28 countries** have called for the project's suspension, citing:

- **Biodiversity**: Sazan is uninhabited and contains habitat for endangered species
- **Marine environment**: Adjacent Karaburun-Sazan marine protected area
- **Precedent**: Luxury development on previously-protected Mediterranean islands
- **Process**: Environmental impact assessments not completed to international standards

The Albanian government's approval over environmental opposition is the **Albanian Pipeline 2 contribution to the Trump-family business architecture** — Albania accepted the reputational cost of overriding environmental opposition in exchange for the project's economic promise and the political-relationship value with the Trump administration.

## The Displacement-Adjacent Pattern

The Sazan Island project fits the broader **displacement-adjacent real estate pattern** that characterizes Kushner's Trump 2 deal-sourcing:

- **Gaza**: "Move the people out and then clean it up" (February 2024 statement); 180-skyscraper master plan (January 2026)
- **Belgrade**: NATO-bombed former military headquarters — memory-of-war displacement
- **Sazan Island**: Uninhabited island but ecological displacement of endangered species
- **Albanian sovereignty**: Private foreign-billionaire ownership of Albanian-national-territory island

Each of these deals involves some form of displacement — of people, memory, habitat, or sovereignty — as a feature of the deal rather than a bug. The Aman brand (ultra-luxury resort operator) is the common commercial positioning across the projects.

## Significance

The April 19, 2026 NYT revelation is significant because:

1. **It converts a speculative Kushner-Qatar relationship claim into a documented partnership** — Ramez Al-Khayyat's on-record confirmation to NYT is the evidentiary anchor
2. **It demonstrates that Affinity partnership structures can preserve operational control while obscuring sovereign-adjacent capital sources** — Sazan Operations was publicly presented without the Qatari ownership layer for months
3. **It extends the Qatar-to-Trump-family capital pattern into Mediterranean Europe** — Qatar's reach via Kushner/Affinity is not confined to Middle East deals but actively expanding geographically
4. **It adds a "private Qatari billionaire" structural layer** between Qatari state capital and U.S.-political-family recipients — providing legal deniability of sovereign involvement while preserving underlying relationship
5. **The project's continued approval despite environmental and ownership concerns** signals that Albanian political will to override opposition is strong — an indicator the project has sovereign-level backing in both Qatar and Albania
