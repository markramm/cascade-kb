On October 7, 2025, the U.S. Senate confirmed **Michael B. Stuart** as **General Counsel of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services**. Stuart becomes the first former U.S. Attorney to hold the role. He resigned from the West Virginia State Senate the same day. President Trump had nominated Stuart in February 2025 (nomination PN25-52).

## Why This Confirmation Matters

Stuart's confirmation is a structurally important event for the HHS-Palantir cluster (see [[hhs-palantir-cluster]]) for three reasons:

1. **Stuart holds the single most comprehensive Palantir exposure pattern** in the eight-appointee HHS cluster — his ProPublica disclosure surfaces Palantir under employment, spouse, *and* transaction categories simultaneously. No other HHS Palantir-connected appointee has all three
2. **The GC portfolio includes review of the CMS-ICE data-sharing agreement litigation** — 20 state attorneys general sued to block the July 2025 data-sharing framework; an August 2025 preliminary injunction was granted; a December 2025 ruling narrowed but did not eliminate permissible sharing. Stuart's confirmation places him in the legal-defense position for this framework going forward
3. **The GC also reviews ethics agreements** for other HHS political appointees, including Clark Minor (CTO/CIO, Palantir alumnus) and the other six HHS Palantir-connected appointees — meaning an appointee with the cluster's most-comprehensive Palantir exposure now has authority to review the ethics compliance of the cluster's other members

## Prosecutorial Background

Stuart's 2018–2021 tenure as U.S. Attorney for the Southern District of West Virginia produced notable prosecutions:

- First-ever prosecution of members of the West Virginia Supreme Court of Appeals
- Largest West Virginia elder fraud takedown in the state's history
- **Largest Medicaid fraud prosecution in the state's history** — directly relevant to HHS's fraud-prevention framing for CMS-ICE data sharing
- Largest West Virginia law enforcement drug takedown

The Medicaid-fraud prosecution background has given the administration's CMS-ICE data-sharing framework professional cover for its fraud-prevention rationale, even as the actual use case extends well beyond fraud investigation into deportation targeting via Palantir's ELITE tool.

## West Virginia Political Context

Sen. Shelley Moore Capito (R-WV) shepherded Stuart through confirmation. Stuart's career prior to the HHS role included:
- Chair of the West Virginia Republican Party
- West Virginia State Senator (Chair, Judiciary Committee)
- Founder of Vandalia Law PLLC
- Partner at Steptoe & Johnson PLLC (Co-Chair, Corporate practice)

His State Senate resignation on the same day as confirmation created a WV Senate vacancy subsequently filled via Governor's appointment.

## Structural Significance

The confirmation completes the HHS-Palantir cluster's personnel architecture at the General Counsel level:

- **Clark Minor** (CTO/CIO, February 13, 2025) — controls technical infrastructure
- **Michael Stuart** (GC, October 7, 2025) — controls legal review of that infrastructure
- **Matthew Buckham** (Acting Chief of Staff, July 2025) — controls day-to-day political coordination
- **Jim O'Neill** (Deputy Secretary, Thiel-Mithril co-founder) — controls senior-executive decision-making

Each of the four operational pivots is held by an appointee with a Palantir- or Thiel-network exposure. See [[hhs-palantir-cluster]].
