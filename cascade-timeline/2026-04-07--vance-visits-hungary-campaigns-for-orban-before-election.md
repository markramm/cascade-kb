Vice President JD Vance made a two-day visit to Budapest on April 7-8, 2026, addressing an election rally at a soccer stadium in an overt campaign to boost Hungarian Prime Minister Viktor Orbán five days before the country's April 12 parliamentary elections. The visit came after Trump endorsed Orbán in February. Vance slammed Ukrainian President Zelenskyy for comments critical of Orbán and accused the European Union of improperly influencing the election.

The visit was accompanied by $1.2 billion in Hungarian purchases: MOL, Hungary's state oil company, announced it would buy $500 million in US crude oil, and the Hungarian army committed to $700 million in HIMARS rocket systems. The timing -- a US Vice President actively campaigning for a foreign leader facing a competitive election, while that leader's government simultaneously signs major defense and energy deals -- represents an extraordinary blurring of diplomacy, commerce, and electoral interference. Orbán's Fidesz party, which has systematically captured Hungary's media, judiciary, and electoral systems during 16 years of rule, has been a model for the authoritarian governance framework promoted by the Trump administration. Vance made the Budapest stop en route to Pakistan for the Iran peace talks in Islamabad, compressing the two priorities of shoring up authoritarian allies and prosecuting the Iran war into a single trip.
