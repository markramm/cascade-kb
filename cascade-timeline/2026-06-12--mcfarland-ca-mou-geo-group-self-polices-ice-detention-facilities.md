# McFarland, CA MOU Lets GEO Group Self-Police Its ICE Detention Facilities

## What Happened

Capital & Main documented (June 12, 2026) that in **McFarland**, a small agricultural town in California's San Joaquin Valley, a **2024 memorandum of understanding between the city and The GEO Group, Inc.** assigns GEO's **civilian administrators** responsibility for specific law-enforcement duties at its two ICE detention facilities — the **Golden State Annex** and the **Central Valley Annex**. Per McFarland police officials and records cited in the reporting, the **McFarland Police Department does not respond to calls** at either facility.

Under the MOU, GEO staff are responsible for detaining people "suspected of violating the law" at the facilities and for processing "evidence obtained from an arrest, including but not limited to weapons, narcotics and currency" — **even though GEO administrators are not sworn police officers**. The reporting documents a case in which a detainee who said he was assaulted at the facility wanted to report it to police, but the department indicated it does not respond to calls there. The facilities are the same ones California state investigators found, in May 2026, to be deteriorating — described in state-inspection reporting as "cruel, inhumane and unacceptable."

## Why It Matters

This is **privatized police power as oversight-elimination** — the detention-lane instance of accountability removal by jurisdictional handoff. The structural move: the public law-enforcement body that would normally take a crime report, preserve evidence, and create an independent record of what happens inside a custodial facility has, by contract, **ceded that function to the private operator of the facility itself**. The entity that runs the detention center is also the entity that "polices" it and processes the evidence of any wrongdoing inside it. There is no external party to whom a detainee can report an assault — the guard's employer is the police.

It is the same accountability-evasion architecture documented elsewhere in the corpus, here achieved through a **municipal MOU** rather than a federal memo: substitute the operator's self-investigation for independent oversight, and abuses inside the facility become structurally invisible because the only "investigator" is the accused. It converges with the New Jersey GEO inspector-access fight ([[2026-06-02--nj-sues-geo-group-delaney-hall-health-inspector-access]]) — both are GEO facilities where the contested question is **who is allowed to look inside** — and with the deadliest-year detention-death pattern ([[2026-06-12--eighteenth-ice-detention-death-of-2026-denny-adan-gonzalez-deadliest-year]]), where the absence of independent oversight is the precondition for unaccountable harm.
